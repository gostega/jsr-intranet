For my journalism project, we asked some Indonesian exchange students what questions they had about Australia. Menarik juga. You can improve your speaking skills by practising answering their questions aloud in Indonesian. (You could even record your answers and listen to them to identify areas for improvement and ways of developing your speaking skills.)

Re: Menasehati..., dari Ibu_Indah pada 08:21.

Ya, menarik sekali Dewi. And a great idea for speaking practice. One further tip:

If you are asked a yes/no question, it is much better to expand on your answer to give further information. For example:

Apakah keluarga Anda makan makanan halal?

Look at the three examples of answers from students below:

  1. Tidak.
  2. Tidak, keluarga saya tidak beragama Islam.
  3. Tidak, keluarga saya tidak beragama Islam. Akan tetapi, kami tahu orang Islam tidak boleh makan daging babi, jadi kami tidak akan menyediakan makanan itu sewaktu Anda menginap di rumah kami.
Which student has done the best job of responding? Explain your reasons.

Student 3. They have not only answered the question asked and given an explanation which demonstrates that they speak Indonesia quite well, they have also addressed the concern behind the question - that is, the concern about eating food that is not halal.
