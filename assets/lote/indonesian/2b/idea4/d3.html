Pe-an dan per-an

Dari Brendon:

I’ve been running into a lot of words that have the affix pe-an or per-an whilst I’ve been preparing for my pekerjaan baru dan pertukaran Dewi (misalnya, pelayanan, pendidikan, penginapan, pengobatan dan lain-lain).

Jadi saya pikir saya seharusnya meningkatkan pengertian saya tentang aspek tata bahasa ini.

From what I can find out, there are basically three main things to understand.

  • Firstly, pe-an and per-an words are nouns.

  • Secondly, if you can identify the base-word, you can guess, or look up, the meaning of the pe-an or per-an word.

  • Thirdly, pe-an words relate to me- verbs (eg menemukan = discover, penemuan = discovery) and per-an words relate to ber- verbs (eg bertemu = meet, pertemuan = meeting).

Now let's look at them individually.

Pe-an

Pe-an words are nouns that usually come from verbs that have the me- prefix. Like me- verbs, pe-an nouns may start with any of the following combinations of letters and relate to the base-word in the same way as me- verbs.

Pe-an words are nouns that usually come from verbs that have the me- prefix. Like me- verbs, pe-an nouns may start with any of the following combinations of letters and relate to the base-word in the same way as me- verbs.

pe- l, m, n, r, w, y
pem- b, f, (p), v
pen- c, d, j, (t)
peng- a, e, i, o, u, g, h, (k)
peny- (s)

Remember, the letters in brackets disappear when the affix is applied!

To get a little more complicated, the pe-an word is often the process or act of doing the base-word.  For example, buka means ‘to open’. Pembukaan is the process of opening something, for example:

Pembukaan sekolah baru itu dihadiri gubernur.

Pengumpulan perangko adalah hobi yang populer di Indonesia.

Per-an

Dari Brendon:

Ok, next bit.

Per-an words are related to ber- verbs, and they are always nouns. For example:

berhenti = to stop        perhentian = a place of stopping (eg bus stop)

It is hard to come up with any rules about per-an, other than that it is always a noun related to the base-word. But here are a couple of ideas:

  • Sometimes the per-an form is the result of the verb, such as perbaikan.

  • Sometimes it is a collection of the base-word, or a place where a collection of the base-word is found. For example, pertokoan means a collection of shops or a shopping centre. A perhentian is a place where buses berhenti.

  • Sometimes it’s both; for example, perkebunan means horticulture, but it can also be a collection of kebun or a plantation.

As with most rules, there are always exceptions! For example, bekerja is a ber- verb, but it doesn’t have the ‘r’ because ‘berkerja’ is very hard to say. Therefore the per-an form is pekerjaan, but it is still classified as a per-an word, because it doesn’t come from a me- verb form.

Here are some examples of per-an words that I’ve heard a lot. Again, there’s a complete list in the pojok bahasa.

pertukaran exchange (eg student exchange, currency)
perhentian bis / pemberhentian bis bus stop (it used to be tempat perhentian bis – place of the stopping of the bus)
perbedaan difference
persamaan similarity
pertokoan shopping centre
perpustakaan library
perusahaan company
perbaikan improvement
perhatian attention
persaudaraan matters relating to family / fraternity / brotherhood
pertimbangan estimation
pertolongan help/assistance
