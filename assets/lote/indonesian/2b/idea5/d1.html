Anjuran

Dari Brendon:
Reproduced with the permission of
Dominic Morice, AusAID

Dewi’s dad’s boss and his wife are coming to Perth. Tampkanya mereka kaya sekali. Their primary reason is to get a medical consultation. The standard of hospitals in Indonesia is not as high as in Australia, so people who can afford it will go abroad if they have serious health problems.

The second reason they are coming is to look at buying an investment property here – another thing a lot of Indonesians do, especially if they have a child who is planning to come to uni here.

Dewi says the third reason they are coming is to shop. Aneh sekali karena I wouldn’t have thought of Perth as an international shopping destination! Just different stuff, I guess. Saya tidak suka berbelanja.

Anyway, they've asked us to act as their tour guides - bagus sekali. It will be good practice for me! Making recommendations to them on where to go and what to see is not so different from making recommendations to exchange students. However, if we are acting as tour guides, we need to be able to justify our recommendations and 'sell' things a bit more, by using descriptive phrases.

There is some stuff in the pojok bahasa you can use to revise language related to anjuran dan keterangan. Berguna sekali.

Brendon, you seem to use sekali a lot. Did you know there are some other words for ‘very’? Using some of these will make your Indonesian sound a lot more sophisticated.

You’ve probably also seen sangat used before the adjective to mean ‘very’ (sangat bagus).

Amat is another word that can be used in the same way as sangat (before the adjective) to mean ‘very’ (amat bagus). It isn’t used that often, but you will hear it sometimes.

You can use all three in combination to say ‘very, very, very something’ (sangat, amat bagus sekali).

Banget is the slang or conversational form of ‘very’ and must only be used in casual conversational contexts (bagus banget!).

Why use more than one word for ‘very’? Well, it is less repetitive to use different forms of ‘very’, and makes your Indonesian sound more interesting – and more advanced! Which is sangat amat bagus sekali!

Jangan lupa! You should also vary the adjectives you choose and not rely on bagus all the time! Try using words such as menarik, hebat, or more specific descriptors.
