Terjemahan

It can be really tempting to use an Internet translator to write things in Indonesian (or other languages), and there are a lot to choose from. Unfortunately, these translators can never understand all the rules and nuances of language.

For example, in trying to explain in Indonesian how to play cricket, Sean used an Internet translator, which came up with the following sentence:
Memukul pesta dansa dengan kelelawar.

The translator didn’t know whether Sean meant pesta dansa as in dance party or bola as in round object, OR whether he meant bet or kelelawar.

Even if Sean had used a dictionary, he would have had to look past the first word given to make sure he was choosing the right one.

For example,

bat (noun) flying nocturnal mammal
(noun) an implement for hitting (eg cricket bat)
(verb) the action of hitting (your turn to bat)		 kelelawar
bet
memukul

You will notice with the example above, that sometimes words can be either nouns or verbs in English. This is something else to consider when choosing a word from the dictionary. One clue that can help you is to remember that in Indonesian:

  • verbs often start with me- or ber-
  • nouns often end in -an.

Tugas

  1. Use the Kamus worksheet to help you think about looking up words that have more than one meaning.
  2. If you know of an online Indonesianâ€“English translator, test out a few sentences that have words with more than one meaning. Use the following steps.
    1. Type in a sentence in English and translate to Indonesian.
    2. Copy and paste the Indonesian and translate to English. Is it the same as your first sentence? Is it good English? Does it make sense?
    3. Try translating a few sentences using this process and see if you can find a really silly translation. Share it with your classmates and teacher.
