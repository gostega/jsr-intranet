What Suci said

Suci said:

  • Nama saya …
  • Umur saya …
  • Agama saya …
  • Hobi saya …

From these sentences, do you think the following inferences are benar or salah?

  1. The owner follows what is owned.

    Benar Benar. Saya after something indicates ownership, so it is ‘my’. If it comes first, it means ‘I’.
    Salah The owner DOES follow what is owned.

  2. There is no single word for ‘is’ in Indonesian.

    Benar Benar. There are a number of words that may be used, but you are more likely to get it right by not using any word for ‘is’ – just leave it out. If you look it up in your dictionary, it will probably tell you to look up the word 'be', which won't help either.
    Salah There are a number of words that may be used, but you are more likely to get it right by not using any word for ‘is’ – just leave it out. If you look it up in your dictionary, it will probably tell you to look up the word ‘be’, which won’t help either.

Tulislah!

Complete the following sentences:

  • Nama saya

  • Umur saya

  • Agama saya

  • Hobi saya

    (Hint: Just use English if you don’t know the Indonesian.)

In Indonesian, the main thing you are talking about comes first in the phrase or sentence. Click on the word that would come first in Indonesian.

For example, if the phrase is ’My dad‘, the main person you are talking about is ‘dad’, so that word comes first in Indonesian.

When you get the answer right, you will be shown the Indonesian.

  1. My mother

    my Salah! You are not talking about yourself. Owner comes after.
    mother Ibu saya.

  2. I am a mother.

    I Saya ibu.
    mother You are not talking about your mother.

  3. I am funny.

    I Saya lucu.
    funny Funny is not a thing.

  4. He is funny.

    he Dia lucu.
    funny Salah!

  5. My friend

    my Salah!
    friend Teman saya.

  6. My friend is funny.

    my Salah!
    friend Teman saya lucu.
    funny Funny is a description.

  7. Tennis player

    tennis Tennis is not the main thing.
    player. Pemain tenis
