What does the order of the words tell you about Indonesian grammar? (Hint: Listen particularly to the order of the words for ‘my name’. Which word comes first?)

Nama comes first, before saya, so the word order is 'name my' instead of 'my name'. The noun or main thing being talked about comes first.


Look at the following pairs of sentences and note when saya means ‘I’ and when it means ‘my’:

  • Nama saya Brack. Saya suka bermain tenis.
  • Nama saya Diego. Saya suka bermain bola basket.

The following rhyming mnemonic may help you:

  • Saya first means ‘I’.
  • Saya second means ‘my’.

Another way of remembering it is: the owner follows what is owned.

Bicaralah!

Listen to the clips again, substituting your name for each speaker’s when you repeat after them.

Choose one of the three introduction options to memorise. You may be able to remember and repeat it now without looking back or re-listening, but you need to see if you can remember it in five minutes time, in half an hour’s time, and tomorrow morning. It is important to keep revising language items regularly in short bursts (rather than one long study session) so that they go into your long-term memory, and not just your short-term memory.
