View the video of Diego introducing himself. Don’t worry about understanding what he is saying. Just get used to listening to the speaker’s voice, pace and accent.

Listen to the clip again twice more (it may help if you close your eyes and concentrate on listening) and focus on any words that sound similar to English.

Now summarise in English as much information as you can about the speaker and then click the Cek jawaban button to reveal the answers.

Did you get any of the following?

  • His name is Diego.
  • He is Indonesian.
  • He lives in Perth.
  • He’s Catholic.
  • His hobbies are guitar and basketball.

If you got several of the answers listed, you probably used a number of the following strategies:

  • listening for key words
  • identifying cognates (recognisable words)
  • inferring (predicting or guessing) meaning from context (surrounding bits)
  • re-listening
  • note-taking.

All these strategies are important to think about when you are viewing, reading or listening to texts with unfamiliar language, whether they are foreign language texts or difficult English language texts.

False friends

Words in Indonesian that resemble English words don’t always mean what you think. For example, cat means paint. Much of the time, though, you can make a good guess, especially if you sound the word aloud and it sounds the same.
