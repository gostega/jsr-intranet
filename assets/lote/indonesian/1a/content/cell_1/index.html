Introduce

This section is an introduction to bahasa Indonesia, designed to ease you into (or back into) Indonesian. Through learning how to make basic personal introductions, you will learn about the sounds and writing system of bahasa Indonesia and the skills involved in learning a language. You will also begin to explore the culture of Indonesia, Australia’s closest neighbour, through learning the language.

Indonesian is considered one of the easiest languages in the world to learn and is spoken by over 220 million people throughout Indonesia. It is also similar to the Malay used in Malaysia and Brunei, so speakers of Indonesian and Malay can communicate without great difficulty.

Here are some reasons why Indonesian is considered to be so easy to learn:

  • Indonesian uses the same alphabet as English (there are no special characters to learn) and Indonesian words are spelled phonetically.
  • The grammar is simple compared to other languages – you will find information on why this is so in the pojok bahasa, which explains some of the grammar.
  • There are a lot of words you will recognise in Indonesian, because they have been adopted from English or other languages, for example televisi, ambisi, informasi, and so on.

Before you begin, read a little about the history of the Indonesian language in the pojok budaya.

You will:

  • learn about the Indonesian language (history and sounds and writing system), culture and people
  • learn some strategies for understanding Indonesian
  • view and listen to simple introductions
  • be able to introduce yourself simply.

Print out the kartu kemajuan to track your progress as you go through this section of work. You should also save it in a folder on your computer.

At the end of this section, you will demonstrate your understanding of a short personal introduction, and introduce yourself in writing or by speaking.

It should take you approximately six hours to complete this section.
