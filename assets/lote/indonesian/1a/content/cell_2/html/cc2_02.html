Keluarga

The second subheading on the biodata (female / male) is Keluarga, as family is very important in Indonesia.

Read more about keluarga in the pojok budaya.

Ayah and Bapak are both used for ‘father’ in Indonesian. Bapak is also used as a polite term of address (like Mr) for men, and Ibu is used the same way for women.

  • You would call your father Bapak, Pak, or Ayah.
  • You would call your (male) teacher Pak Davis, Pak Ari, or whatever his name is, or just Pak.
  • You would address a man you don't know, or whose name you may have forgotten, as Pak, or ask his name by saying Siapa nama Bapak?

Saudara refers to siblings, but is also used as a polite term of address for young people. Saudari is also used, but only for young women.

Anak ke: 2 dari 4 saudara (or Anak kedua dari empat saudara) means ‘The second of four children’. It is very easy to say ‘second, third etc.’ in Indonesian â€“ all you do is add a ke- to the number (ketiga = 3rd, keempat = 4th). The only exception is first, which is pertama. For example:

  • Saya anak pertama dari dua (ber)saudara.
  • Saya anak ketiga dari tiga (ber)saudara.

Jumlah saudara: 3 bersaudara means the person has three brothers/sisters. Ber- often means ‘has’ (eg bernama, berumur, berarti).

Read the following sentence.

Nama ayah Nuri adalah Putu Aryana, dan nama ibunya Wahyu Abidin. Nuri ada dua saudara.

Jot down a translation of the sentence in English.

The name of Nuri’s father is Putu Aryana and the name of his mother is Wahyu Abidin. Nuri has 2 siblings.


The suffix -nya can be added to a word to make it his/hers/theirs/its. For example:
Ibunya = his/her mother.

As well as saying nama ibunya, you can also say:
Ibunya bernama … His mother has the name …
