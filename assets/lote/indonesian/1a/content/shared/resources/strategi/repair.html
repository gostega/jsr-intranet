Repair strategies

Don’t be afraid to ask for help. Most native speakers are more than happy to help you out if they can. Of course many people don’t speak English, so you need to be able to ask for (and cope with) help in Indonesian. Know a range of ‘repair strategies’ – phrases such as ‘please repeat’, ‘please slow down’, ‘what does (word) mean?’

Some useful repair strategies when speaking include:

Apa artinya … ? What is the meaning of … ?
Apa artinya … dalam bahasa Indoneisa/Inggris? What is the meaning of … in Indonesian/English?
Tolong ulangi. Please repeat that.
Pelan-pelan. Please slow down.
Tolong ulangi pelan-pelan. Please repeat that slowly.
Maaf, saya kurang mengerti. I'm sorry, I don’t really understand.

You should try to memorise these phrases.

Expressions and exclamations

Expressions and exclamations can greatly enhance your communication. They are rather hard to translate, and different people use different expressions, so the translations below are meant to give you a sense of how and when you might use these expressions.

Wah! Wow!
Ampun! Oh my God! (literally: forgiveness)
Astaga! Wow! Damn! Crikey!
Lho! Really?! Wow! (general exclamation of surprise)
Aduh! Oh dear!
Sayang … What a pity …
