Making meaning

When you are faced with words you don't understand, you can often guess what they mean. You already use a lot of strategies to predict meaning in your first language without realising it. There are a number of things you can do to make meaning in a foreign language:

  • Be aware that there are a lot of cognates (words taken from English, with the spelling or sound altered slightly) in Indonesian that are easy to guess, such as informasi.
  • You can use clues from the context or setting to help you guess the meaning of the word or phrase. Clues include the words around the words you don't know, or visual clues such as pictures, facial expression or actions.
  • In conversations, you can ask someone the meaning of a word, to explain using different words, to repeat themselves or slow down.
  • Note down key words that seem important or are repeated and look them up later.

The main thing is not to panic or give up. You should also read (and keep re-reading) the strategies specific to viewing, reading, writing, listening and speaking to help you as you attempt each task.
