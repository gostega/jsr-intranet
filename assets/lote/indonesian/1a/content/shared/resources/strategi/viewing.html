Viewing strategies

There is an enormous amount of information your brain has to process in even a very short segment of video. The following strategies will help you.

Relax. You won't understand it all on the first viewing, so don't stress about it. You'll get better as you go along.

Ask yourself:

  • What is the text-type?
  • Who is the text aimed at?
  • What is the purpose of the text?

View the text a few times.

  • The first time, look for visual clues that help you guess the meaning of what is going on.
  • The second, third and fourth time, listen carefully for aural clues (this can include spoken words, music, or sound effects). Jot down key words and phrases.
  • Listen for key words. Jot them down in Indonesian as fast as you can. Don't try to translate them – it will take too long and you will miss the next few key words or clues.
  • After you have watched a few times, go over your notes and look up any words you think are important and that you are unsure of.
  • Enjoy watching!
