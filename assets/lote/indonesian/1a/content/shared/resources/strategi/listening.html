Listening and speaking strategies

General

  • It takes you a while to get used to someone's voice – accent, mannerisms, style and pace of delivery – especially in a foreign language. Give yourself time to tune in and adjust to the speaker.
  • If you are listening to a recording (for example recorded message, song), listen to it all at once, without stopping, to get a general impression. Then go back and replay it in chunks, making notes as you go.
  • Listen for key words and words you already know.
  • Use other aural information such as the tone of voice and inflection to help you guess information (for example whether that was a question or a statement, how the speaker is feeling, and so on).
  • Guess or infer meaning. Do not look up every word, or ask every word.

Note-making while listening

If you are listening to an audio text such as a radio report, make notes in Indonesian. It is easier for your brain to process one language at a time. Try the following strategy to see if it works for you.

  • Listen to the whole text, making notes in Indonesian.
  • Listen again in sections, adding notes in Indonesian.
  • Go back over your notes, and make sense of what you have written down. Jot down key points in your first language.
  • Listen again and fill in any gaps and confirm your interpretation.

Conversation

  • Tarik nafas! Jangan gugup! Take a few deep breaths. Don't be nervous.
  • Predict some questions you may be asked beforehand, and practise your answers aloud.
  • Listen for the question words used, as they tell you something about the question and response.

Appropriate language

Think about the register (or level of language) that is appropriate to the conversation. This depends on your relationship with the speaker and the context of conversation, for example whether it is a job interview or phone call to a mate.

Consider which form of 'you' and 'I' you are going to use – Anda/saya, kamu/aku, lu/gue. NEVER use kamu with someone older than you or someone you don't know very well. Remember, you can always ask the person you are speaking to how you should address them.

Saya kurang pasti, apakah saya harus pakai istilah 'Anda' atau 'Bapak/Ibu', atau 'kamu', atau sesuatu yang lain. I don't know if I should use the term Anda, or Bapak/Ibu, or kamu, or something else.

You may find it useful to use a disclaimer somewhere in the conversation. For example:

Maaf, saya kurang lancar bahasa Indonesia. Jangan tersinggung kalau saya berkata sesuatu yang kurang pas. I'm sorry, I'm not too fluent in Indonesian. Please don't be offended if I say something that is not quite right.
Maaf, saya belum pintar bahasa Indonesia. Sorry, I'm not yet that good at Indonesian.
