Asli mana?

As well as over 17 000 islands, there are over 700 languages and 300 ethnic groups in Indonesia. A song by the popular Indonesian comedy group Project Pop says:

Ada orang Batak, ada orang Jawa

Ada orang Ambon, ada juga orang Padang

Ada orang Menado, ada orang Madura

Ada orang Papua, (yang) nggak disebut jangan marah

Ethnic identity is very important to Indonesians. The question 'berasal dari mana?' is frequently asked in introductions. Similarly, 'orang mana?' or 'asli mana?' is often asked to determine ethnic group. A person's ethnic identity does not always match with where they are living, as many people move between regions for economic reasons, for example finding work in major cities. As Java is one of the most densely populated places in the world, the Indonesian government implemented a scheme called transmigrasi, whereby people (sometimes whole villages) were offered financial incentives to move to the lesser populated outer islands.

To get an idea of the diversity of Indonesia (Indonesia's national motto is Bhinneka Tunggal Ika), watch this promotional advertisement produced by the Ministry of Culture and Tourism.
