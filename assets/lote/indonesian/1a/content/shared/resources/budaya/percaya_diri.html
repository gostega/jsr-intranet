Percaya diri

Boasting, bragging, or building yourself up is considered to be extremely poor manners in Indonesia. Instead, it is more usual to downplay your skills, for example by saying 'saya belum pintar bahasa Indonesia' or 'saya belum lancar bahasa Indonesia'. If you were to say, 'Saya jago bermain bola basket', you would likely get the response:

Aduh, GR sekali!

GR actually stands for gede rasa, which is originally a Javanese phrase meaning 'big feeling'.

The moral of the story is: rendah hati, jangan sombong.

The word sombong can be translated as 'conceited', but it is really more like 'haughty', or 'snobbish'. It implies that you think you are too good to talk to other people. When you are walking around in Indonesia, people always want to talk to you – whether to just chat, ask where you are from, or sell you something. If you walk past without responding, you may hear 'Wah, sombong sekali!', so it's better to give a quick polite response before moving on.
