Sudah mandi?

In English, it's common to say 'Hi, how are you?' as a way of starting a conversation. In Indonesian, when you live in a boarding house or with a family, people will often start a conversation with 'Sudah mandi?', particularly early morning or late afternoon. Indonesians mandi dua kali sehari. While it sounds a bit funny, it is really just a way of opening a conversation. If you say 'Belum', you may get the joking response 'Aduh, bau'. You may also be asked 'Sudah makan, belum?' as a polite way of starting a conversation.

If you are walking down the street, you will often be asked 'Mau ke mana?'. It is not thought to be nosey, rather as showing an interest in you. Indonesians are very friendly, and the concept of jam karet is very strong, so people always have time for a chat.
