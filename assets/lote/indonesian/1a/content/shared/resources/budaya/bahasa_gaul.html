Bahasa gaul

Bahasa gaul literally means 'language of socialisation'. There are a variety of ideas regarding what bahasa gaul actually is. If you ask different people, you will find some consider it to be everyday language, some consider it to be youth language, some consider it to be slang, and some consider it to be an area-specific variety of Indonesian. It used to be that bahasa gaul was considered to be Jakartanese, but, with the spread of media technology, much of bahasa gaul is used and understood across Indonesia instead of being restricted to one area. Whatever it is, bahasa gaul is constantly evolving and changing with the times, just as the English language (both formal and slang) changes as new terms are coined for new technology, new hobbies and new attitudes.

One word that started off as bahasa gaul but is used so much that it has become 'standard Indonesian' is the word nggak, or 'no'. Read the following article from the journal Inside Indonesia on youth language.

It is very important to remember that the use of bahasa gaul depends on context. It is used between close friends and never in a formal situation. These are not terms you should be practising with your teacher.
