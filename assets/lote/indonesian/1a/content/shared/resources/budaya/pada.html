Pada siang hari …

Reproduced with the permission of the Department
of Culture and Tourism, Indonesia and Mikhail
Tsyganov.

Instead of being split at midday into morning and afternoon, times of the day in Indonesian follow more the pattern of the sun and the weather it generates. Pagi starts when the sun comes up, and malam starts when it goes down. Siang is the hottest part of the day. People often rest or sleep after lunch to give them the energy to get through the rest of the day. They may then stay up later than people who don't have a rest, and are more active when the sun has gone down and the weather is cooler. Sore is the time from when you wake up from your siesta until sunset.

Pagi, siang, sore and malam are important words to remember. You will see them again and again in the context of meals (makan siang) and times of day (pada pagi hari).

Selamat literally means safe. Instead of wishing someone a good morning, you wish them a safe morning. If two people know each other well or the situation is casual, they may drop the selamat or shorten it to slamat or mat.

Time is usually written numerically, using the 24-hour clock. For example, 16:00 = 4 pm.

Indonesia has three time zones, one each for Western, Central and Eastern Indonesia. These are called Waktu Indonesia Barat (WIB), Waktu Indonesia Tengah (WITA), and Waktu Indonesia Timur (WIT) respectively.

The word pukul or jam are used to state the time. Therefore, you may see or hear pukul lima or jam lima used for five o'clock. Pukul is abbreviated to Pk, therefore Pk 17:00 = 5 pm.
