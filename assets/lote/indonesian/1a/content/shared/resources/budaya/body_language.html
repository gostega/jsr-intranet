Body language

Body language is an important part of communication. When you are speaking to someone, their body language may tell you whether they are listening to you or understanding you, whether they are interested, and whether they are feeling positive or negative about what you are saying.

Besides the softer handshake typical of Indonesian culture, there are a number of other aspects of body language that may be interpreted differently from what you are used to. Here are some common examples of body language:

  • Standing with your hands on your hips is a sign of anger.
  • Using your left hand to indicate or hand someone something is considered very kasar.
  • Smiling can be used to cover anger or embarrassment.
  • Pointing with your index finger is rude in some areas; people more commonly point with their thumb.

With all its different ethnic and cultural groups, there are differences in body language across Indonesia. Not everyone will avoid your eyes as a sign of respect. Not everyone will avoid pointing with their index finger. However, these are all common things to be aware of.
