Personal pronouns

Here are some words for 'you' and 'I'. They are important terms to commit to memory.

Word Translation Used:
saya I /me/my
  • when talking to someone older or someone you don't know well
  • with someone in a position of authority, such as your teacher
  • in formal situations or texts, eg interviews, formal letters.
aku
  • talking to friends
  • in casual situations.
ku
  • talking to friends or in casual situations
  • when you want to say 'my …' in casual situations
  • for 'I' in songs or poetry.
gue
  • by teenagers, especially those in cities, with close friends
  • in teen magazines or TV shows
  • in Jakarta
  • abbreviated as gw in SMS.
Anda you/your/yours
  • in formal situations
  • when you are talking to someone you don't know well
  • when showing respect to someone.
kamu
  • talking to friends or in casual situations
  • talking to people younger or less important than you (eg small children)
  • abbreviated as mu for possessive 'your'.
engkau
or
'kau
  • in a very close relationship.
elu/lu/lo
  • as slang by teenagers in cities
  • in youth magazines and television shows
  • as slang in Jakarta
  • as an insult.
