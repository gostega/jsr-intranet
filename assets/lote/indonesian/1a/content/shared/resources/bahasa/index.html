Resources – Bahasa

The pojok bahasa is a resource for you to find information about bahasa Indonesia – primarily grammar and vocabulary, but some other useful information. You can refer to it whenever you need to. It is a good place to check for information before you go to a dictionary, the Internet or your teacher.

Tata bahasa means 'parts of language'. This section contains useful information about the forms and features of language, such as grammar. You can refer to it at any time.
Indonesian grammar is very simple. For example:

  • Tenses are easy. Verbs don't change with tense (eg throw/throwing/thrown). You just add a word saying 'will' or 'currently', or 'have already' before the verb.
  • It is easy to form sentences. It may take you a long time to be able to make a whole sentence in another language, but in Indonesian you can progress a lot faster.
  • Plurals are easy. The word doesn't change – it just has a number or a word such as 'many' or 'few' added in front of it to indicate quantity if necessary. For example, student(s) = murid; person/people = orang.
  • There are no masculine/feminine objects as there are in some European languages, such as French and German.

Kosa kata is a set of documents that you can save on your own computer. It has lists of words from each section of work that you can add to, or you can remove words you already know.
