Did you notice …

Noticing things that are new, or things that you don’t quite understand, is an important learning strategy. You should make notes whenever you ‘notice’ something, so you can look it up or ask questions about it. Some things you may have noticed in Diego’s clip include the fact that he:

  • drops some prefixes
  • uses a number of ber- words.

Why is this?

Dropping of prefixes

In spoken Indonesian, especially in casual contexts (such as chatting with friends), prefixes such as me- and ber- are often dropped, and just the base-word used.

Did you notice that Diego shortened the Indonesian word ‘to play’ from bermain to main?

Verb affixes

Because Diego is discussing what he likes to do, he uses a number of action words, or verbs. You may remember that verbs in Indonesian often start with me- or ber-, but they can also start with di-. Di- is similar to the me- prefix, but simpler to add to or remove from a word. All you do is take the di- off the front.

You may also hear or see words with a meng- prefix having only the me- dropped. For example, ngomong instead of omong, ngomel instead of omel.

Karena

You may have noticed Diego using the word karena, which has come up before as a link word in the kosa kata. You will see and hear this word used frequently and may have even already used it yourself. Being able to give a reason or make a justification is an indicator of your language skills being at a higher level. So karena is a good word to memorise and practise using, if you haven’t already done so. Try writing three sentences using ‘karena’.
