Mainan

Games are very useful for helping you learn words and phrases. One game is something you may know as ‘Memory’. You can play this with a partner or by yourself.

Deal out a set of cards (which has matching pairs) face down. Turn two cards over. If they match, put them to one side. If they don't, turn them face down again and choose another two. Keep going until you have matched all the pairs. The winner is the person with the most pairs.

To play this for language learning, one half of each pair of cards has the word in Indonesian, and the matching card has the word in English (or your first language) or a picture.

Print off the worksheet on heavy paper and cut out all the cards with the descriptions from the song. Shuffle and deal them out face down, then play 'Memory' with them.

Tantangan

Think of six more two-word descriptions (for example, long legs). Write them in Indonesian and in English on the blank cards provided and shuffle them into the pack.
