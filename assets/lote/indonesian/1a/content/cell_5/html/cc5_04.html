Cari kata

You have already learnt about base-words and affixes, and you know that you need to find the base-word to be able to find a word in the dictionary. Practising finding the base-words of these words from the song will help you understand it better, as well as developing your understanding of Indonesian grammar.

Affixes that form verbs include: me-, di-, -i, -kan, ber-.

Affixes that form nouns include: ke-an, pe-an, per-an.

  1. Practise finding the base-word of the following words from the song by typing it in the box. (Please use lower-case letters).
  2. When you have the base-word correct, the meaning of the full affixed word (the word in the first column) will appear once you press the Cek jawaban button.
Nouns Base-word Meaning  
perbedaan difference Remove the per-an. 
perasaan feeling(s) Remove the pe-an. 
persatuan unity Incorrect. Remove the per-an. 
Verbs Base-word Meaning  
mengakui acknowledge Incorrect. Base-word starts with vowel. Remove -i. 
diajari taught Incorrect. Base-word starts with vowel. Remove -i. 
diciptakan created Incorrect. Remember to remove -kan.
 
disebut mentioned Incorrect. Remember to remove di-. 
menyatukan unify/unite Incorrect. Don’t forget the silent ‘s’ with meny-. 
dilakukan carried out Incorrect. Remember to remove -kan. 
diprovokasikan provoked Incorrect. Remember to remove -kan. 

Ejaan non-baku

There are a number of words in the song that are not spelled with standard ‘correct’ spelling. Just as Internet chat and SMS have their own spelling, acronyms and words, bahasa Indonesia has its own modified language, known as bahasa gaul.

Mostly these are just shortened spelling. Often the first part of the prefix is dropped, or some vowels are dropped. It is pretty easy to guess what the standard, formal word is to look up.

ngaku mengaku
ngerti mengerti
kalo kalau
smua semua
cakep cakap
skolahan sekolahan
