The Internet and HP (handphones or mobiles) have made communication and networking much easier. The cost of sending an SMS or renting some time in a warnet (Internet warung or shop) are relatively cheap. This means that it is not only the rich people who are able to communicate messages and ideas, access information and advertise. Technology has effectively narrowed the gap between rich and poor. It also enables communication between different communities.

HP technology is more advanced and widely used in Indonesia than it is in Australia. It is cheaper to own a HP than a computer, so people do their banking, buy and sell things, book tickets and so on using SMS. It is also more widely used because most people don't have landlines. Most Indonesian HPs are prepaid not post-paid, because many people don't have set home addresses. Phone credits are called pulsa.
