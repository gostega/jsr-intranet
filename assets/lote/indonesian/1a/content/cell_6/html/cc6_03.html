Anak bikers

Motorbikes or scooters are far more popular than cars in Indonesia, as they are much cheaper. The average Indonesian family could never dream of affording a car; however, rich families may have several, and employ full-time, live-in drivers. You will often see a family of four or more riding the one motorbike together.

The legal age for driving in Indonesia is 17, but it is very common to see anak remaja much younger than that driving a sepeda motor or motor bebek, especially around areas where there are no polantas. If they are unlucky enough to be picked up by an orang polisi, it is sometimes just a matter of paying a small on-the-spot fine to have the matter forgotten.
