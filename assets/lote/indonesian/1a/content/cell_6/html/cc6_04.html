Nomor

You should already know your numbers in Indonesian, to at least 99. The Indonesian currency uses much higher numbers than the Australian currency. Even if you bought a packet of gum, you would probably be working in thousands of rupiah. Therefore it is very important to know all the numeric classifiers (tens, hundreds, thousands, millions).

belas classifier for teens sebelas, dua belas, tiga belas
puluh classifier for tens sepuluh, dua puluh, tiga puluh
ratus classifier for hundreds seratus, dua ratus, tiga ratus
ribu classifier for thousands seribu, dua ribu, tiga ribu
juta classifier for millions sejuta, dua juta, tiga juta …

If you need a refresher on basic numbers, click here.

The number system is really quite easy.

Remember:

When 'one' is with a classifier (eg 10, 11, 100, 1000) the prefix 'se-' is used (eg sepuluh) rather than the word satu. (Like saying a hundred instead of one hundred.)

When the number 'one' is alone, or last, it is satu.

'And' is not used ('one hundred and three' is just seratus tiga).

Uang

If at today's rate one Australian dollar is 8,812 Indonesian rupiah, the number would be said as follows:

Rp 8,812

(Eight thousand, eight hundred and twelve.)

At this exchange rate, AUD$150 would be:

Rp 1,321,489

(One million, three hundred and twenty-one thousand, four hundred and eighty-nine.)

 Sejuta, tiga ratus dua puluh satu ribu, empat ratus delapan puluh sembilan.
