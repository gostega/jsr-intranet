Naik apa? Jam berapa?

Once you have asked someone out, you need to work out the details. Carefully read the following information, then try having (or scripting) a conversation. Continue on from the conversation you had asking someone out.

Naik apa?

Reproduced with the permission of the Department of Culture and Tourism, Indonesia, and Bambang Wijanarko.

Naik apa? literally means 'going on what?'. You could say '(Kita) naik apa ke sana?' ; however, remember that, in conversation, words like 'you' and 'I' often get dropped. It is usually obvious who you are speaking about, so the words aren't necessary.

If you are asked 'naik apa?', the response is usually 'naik' (plus form of transport). For example:

Suci:       Kita naik apa ke sana?

Diego:    Naik kereta api.

Here are a few forms of transport you may choose.

  • taksi
  • sepeda motor
  • bis
  • mobil
  • kereta api
  • berjalan kaki

Hati-hati!

Naik literally means to go up or to get on something. You can use it for any form of transport – but not walking. So while you would say:

  • Saya naik kereta api

if you wanted to say you were walking, you would leave the naik out:

  • Saya berjalan kaki.

Jam berapa?

Pada jam …

You already know that to say two o'clock, you just need to say 'jam dua' or 'pukul dua'.

Pada is used as in/at/on for time.

If you want to say 'at two-ten', you use the word lewat: Pada jam dua lewat sepuluh.

After half past, it is more normal to say 'ten minutes to three' rather than 'two-fifty'. So ten to three (2:50) would be pada jam tiga kurang sepuluh.

Half past is also more commonly said as 'half to' (the next hour), instead of half past. For example, 2:30 is jam setengah tiga. If there is confusion, you can always say:

Maksudnya jam dua lewat tigapuluh?

Bicaralah!

Practise having the following conversation with a friend. If you are person A, fill in the gaps with your own information. If you are person B, note down the details (place, time and transport) and check whether they were correct. Then reverse roles.

A: Hai, apa kabar?
B: Baik-baik saja. Apa kabar?
A: Baik terima kasih. Hei, ada rencana besok?
B: Belum. Mengapa?
A: Mau ikut ke ________?
B: Boleh. Jam berapa?
A: Pada jam __________.
B: Baiklah. Kita naik apa ke sana?
A: Naik ______________.
