Mau ke mana?

Brainstorm a list of places you may ask a friend to go to with you (eg the movies). Then choose five places to look up the Indonesian words for. You will find a few tempat in the kosa kata.

  1. Mau ke mana hari ini?

    Saya mau ke hari ini.

  2. Mau ke mana besok?

    Saya besok.

  3. Mau ke mana pada akhir minggu?

Ke mana-mana means 'anywhere'. Saya tidak mau ke mana-mana

Lalu …

Using your sentences from the last exercise, or making up new ones, write a paragraph (or two or three extended sentences) describing where you are going. Use the following link words.

  • lalu
  • kemudian
  • sesudah itu
  • sebelum itu

Indicating intention

There are a few alternative words you may hear, see or use instead of mau, including the following.

  • ingin
  • akan
  • harus

Bicaralah!

In Indonesian, have a conversation with a friend and practise asking someone to go somewhere with you.

Mau ke mana?

Where are you going?

Aku mau ke …

I want to go to …

Mau ke ... ?

Do you want to go to … ?

Mau ikut ke ... ?

Do you want to come with me/us to … ?

Boleh

Sure

Maaf, tidak bisa.

Sorry, I can't.

Saya harus ke …

I have to go to …

Remember, it is polite to start your conversations with a greeting and a 'how are you?'

Whenever you come across some new phrases or words, you should spend at least five minutes just trying to memorise them. When you have committed them to your short-term memory, leave them for a while (a few hours or a day) and then go back and see what you can remember. By repeating the process a few times, you will be able to commit the phrases to your longer-term memory.

For more tips on committing language to memory, check the strategi section.
