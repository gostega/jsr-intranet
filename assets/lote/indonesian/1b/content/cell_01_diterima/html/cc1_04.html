Did you notice in the form that there were two groups of people who had special sections to fill out? Can you spot the two groups of people who are differentiated between in this form?

Firstly, Chinese nationals come under extra scrutiny due to different aspects of Indonesia's history, such as the communist coup of 1965 and ongoing racial tensions resulting from the Dutch treatment of Chinese in Indonesia. Even carrying any materials with Chinese characters on them used to be forbidden. For more information, read the section on the Chinese in Indonesia which you may have seen previously.

Secondly, the form assumes that women will be accompanying a working husband, not that they will be travelling independently or working in their own right.

In Indonesia, the husband is still considered the breadwinner of the family, and many places will prioritise men for promotions or have a pay scale which pays men more than women.

Visa application completed in full with 1 (one) recent passport size photo attached.

Original passport valid for more than 6 (six) months beyond the intended length of stay.

Letter of sponsor from a relative, counterpart or family in Indonesia.

Copy of round trip airline ticket/itinerary.

Visa fee AUD$ 60.00 in form of money order or cheque payable to the Indonesian Consulate.
