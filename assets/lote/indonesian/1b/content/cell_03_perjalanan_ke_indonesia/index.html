Daniel and Trish negotiate their first flight to Indonesia and arrival at the airport. On the plane, they listen to announcements and chat with flight attendants and fellow passengers. They listen to the pilot's announcement, watch the in-flight Garuda promotion, read the kartu petunjuk in the seat pocket, and order drinks and their breakfast. Daniel realises he has forgotten his camera and gets a lesson on 'ter-' from the passenger next to him.

You will develop listening strategies to be able to understand conversations and other audio texts. You'll also learn about the affixes ter- and ke-an, and be able to listen for base-words so that you are better able to work out their meaning. These affixes will also help you identify the meaning of various signs you will see around Indonesian airports and bus and train stations. After listening to various phrases used in ordering food and drinks, you will be able to open conversations politely, order your own meal and make specific requests.

You will:

  • do some further research to work out where you want to go in Indonesia
  • watch the Garuda video to develop your understanding of Indonesian national identity and ke-an words
  • listen to the captain's announcement and identify key information
  • read and interpret the kartu petunjuk in the seat pocket
  • listen to food and drinks orders, view a menu and make your own order
  • listen to Trish's conversation with a fellow passenger and learn another way ter- can change a base-word
  • read an article from Garuda Magazine and discuss it with a fellow passenger.

Print out the kartu kemajuan to track your progress as you go through this section of work. You should also save it in a folder on your computer.

It should take you approximately seven hours to complete this section.
