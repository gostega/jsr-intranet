From the man's explanation to Daniel, what do you understand 'ter-' to mean?

Ter- means something is done 'tidak sengaja' (not deliberately) or accidentally. (This is not always the case. Ter- can mean several other things, for example 'the most'.)

Explain the difference between meninggalkan and tertinggal.

Meninggalkan means to leave something somewhere on purpose, and tertinggal means 'left behind accidentally'.

Listen to the conversation again. List the three other ter- words used as examples and their meaning (you can guess the meaning or look it up). Type them into the boxes below.

tertidur – fell asleep
terjatuh – fell accidentally
terbangun – woke suddenly/accidentally

Tulislah!

Practise using the ter- affix. Use five ter- words. You can put them into a paragraph, or a conversational context, or just write individual sentences. You can use the words on this page (there are a few more below) or find other ones.

Note the word order used – Kamera tertinggal. The object that is 'ter-verbed' comes first.

You may wish to listen back to the other examples to check how they are used.

Another example is:

'Minta minuman lagi Bu. Minuman saya terminum penumpang lain'. ('May I have another drink please, miss? My drink was mistakenly drunk by another passenger'.)
