Awalan dan akhiran (Prefixes and suffixes)

Awal = beginning/early

Awalan = thing that comes first = a prefix (something that goes on the start of a word)

Akhir = end

Akhiran = thing that comes last = a suffix (something that goes on the end of a word)

Affixes (prefixes and suffixes) are very important in Indonesian, as they can help you figure out where to find a word in the dictionary and its meaning.

Most importantly, they can help you know whether a word is a noun (a thing or object) or a verb. In English, you can tell if a word is a noun if you can put 'the' 'a' or 'an' in front of it. For example, 'experience'. You can say 'an experience', therefore you know that the word is a noun.

Words that could be nouns or verbs

When you are looking up an English word to find the Indonesian, there are often a lot of choices. One thing you need to ask yourself is what sort of word you are looking for, for example a noun or a verb? Some words in English can be either. For example:

  • Experience, as in 'you will experience problems this week' is a verb.
  • Experience, as in 'the experience will strengthen you' is a noun.
  • Show can also be a noun or a verb ('watching a show', or 'to show something').

In Indonesian, affixes can indicate whether the word is a noun or a verb.

  • Words with me- and ber- prefixes are verbs.
  • Words with -an suffixes are nouns.

So if you want to say 'I want to visit my friend', you'll be looking for a word beginning with me- or ber-. If you want to say 'It was a nice visit', you'll be looking for an -an suffix.

Try translating the following sentences.

  • I went to visit my cousins in New Zealand. It was a nice visit.
  • She hit the ball. It was a hard hit.

Hati-hati! There are exceptions – the word you are looking for may just be a base-word. The best way to find out is to find the example sentence closest to yours in the dictionary.
