Di- verbs

Di- is similar to the me- prefix, but there are two key differences:

  • di- is simpler to add to or remove from a word, as the base-word doesn't change at all
  • di- verbs are usually used with what are called passive (or object focus) sentences.

Look at the difference between these pairs of words:

menangkap to catch ditangkap caught (by)
melempar to throw dilempar thrown (by)
membeli to buy dibeli bought (by)
memukul to hit dipukul hit (by)

A passive (object focus) sentence is when the sentence structure looks like this:

Something (an object) is / will be / is being / has been done (by someone).

For example:

Bola ditangkap oleh regu lain.

Barang ini sudah dibayar.

You will learn more about this sentence structure as you progress in your study of Indonesian.
