Catatan tentang makanan

Nasi

Nasi is the staple food in Indonesia. There is a saying, 'belum makan nasi, belum makan'. Many people eat rice three times a day. Most commonly white rice is eaten with side dishes of sayur-sayuran, daging or telur and, of course, sambal. Nasi goreng or mie goreng is considered a whole meal in itself and is not generally eaten with side dishes.

The importance of rice in Indonesia can be seen from the number of words for rice. Nasi is cooked rice; beras is uncooked rice; padi is the rice plant or unhusked rice; and sawah is a wet rice field.

Makanan pencuci mulut

You will notice that the menu is not split into courses of entree, mains and sweets, as this is a Western idea, including the idea of eating mains before sweets. If you took a cake to an Indonesian home, it would be more likely that it would be served first as a sign of respect to the visitor, than last as dessert. Even the notion of sweet versus savoury food is strange in Indonesia. Cheese is often served on sweet foods (such as doughnuts) and sausage rolls scattered with chocolate sprinkles may be found in bakeries in Java.

Fresh fruit is often used to finish a meal as a makanan pencuci mulut.

No thank you

When you say 'no thank you' in Indonesian, you say 'terima kasih tidak'. Sometimes the 'tidak' is dropped from the end. If you offer someone something and they say 'thank you', they are more likely to be refusing.
