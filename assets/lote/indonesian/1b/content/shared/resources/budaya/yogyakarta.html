Yogyakarta

Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat

Reproduced with the
permission of
Richard Charlwood

Daerah Istimewa Yogyakarta (commonly known as Yogya) is considered the cultural capital of Indonesia. It actually became the capital of Indonesia for five years during the nationalist struggle against the Dutch. The royal Javanese sultanate of Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat still governs the region, and the royal palace, or Kraton (sometimes spelled keraton), is located there and receives hundreds of tourists every year.

The Kraton is a walled complex of approximately one square kilometre, around which the city of Yogyakarta has grown. Within the walls there is the actual palace itself, an alun-alun with symbolic banyan trees, Tamansari (a water palace and former recreational facility for the Sultan's family), a pasar burung, and numerous houses for the Kraton staff. There is a rule that no building in the Kraton area may be taller than the Kraton itself. For more information on the Kraton, look under 'places of interest' (Kraton, Tamansari, and Alun-alun Kidul) on www.yogyes.com/

Jalan Malioboro

To the north of the Kraton is the famous shopping street and tourist centre of Malioboro. Jalan Malioboro is bustling with life at any time of the day or night. From the early hours of the morning warung tenda are set up to serve bubur ayam and other breakfast foods. By nine o'clock, the pavements between the shops and the road are lined with market stalls selling handicrafts, clothing, watches and food. Side streets are full of kaki lima and becak drivers looking for custom. Waiting patiently on the sides of the main street ignoring the numerous motorbikes, cars and buses, are horse-drawn carriages.

At the south end of Jl. Malioboro on the west side of the street is Pasar Beringhardjo, a large traditional market selling everything from sanggul to fresh fruit and traditional snacks. Further up on the same side of the street is the modern Mal Malioboro, which houses a supermarket, a couple of department stores and designer-label boutiques.

North Yogya

To the north of Jalan Malioboro is the train line which runs east to west. A little further north is Jalan Solo, one of the main roads connecting Yogyakarta with other cities, including the second royal city of Solo (formerly Surakarta) to the northeast. Further north still on Jalan Kaliurang, which leads up to the village of Kaliurang on the side of Gunung Merapi (the volcano which overshadows Yogya), is the famous Universitas Gajah Mada which hosts ACICIS, Australia's largest in-country Indonesian study program.
