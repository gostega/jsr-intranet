Sepatu atau sandal?

Sepatu is literally translated as shoes. So what is the difference between sepatu and sandal? Aren't sandal and sandal jepit shoes?

Sepatu in Indonesia generally refers to closed-in shoes, which are considered formal. Sandal are more for casual wear around the house.

In Indonesian culture, the feet are considered 'unclean'. Muslims take their shoes off and wash their feet before praying. You take your shoes off before entering an Indonesian home. It is considered rude to sit with one foot propped on your knee, and very rude to point to anything with your foot. Throwing your shoes at someone is considered a terrible insult.

The head is considered the most holy part of the body as it is closest to heaven. It is treated with respect. It is very rude to pat someone on the head, especially if they are older than you and definitely never do it with your left hand, which is considered 'dirty'.
