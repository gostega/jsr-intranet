Teh atau kopi?

Tea and coffee are served quite differently in Indonesia. While you can get cappucino in shopping malls in cities, milk is generally not used in tea or coffee. Raising cows requires a lot of space that is not available in Indonesia. Java is one of the most densely populated areas in the world and it is mountainous, so it is not conducive to raising cattle. Many Indonesians are actually lactose intolerant and cannot drink milk.

Tea is always served black in a tall glass and is usually sweetened. Central Javanese are particularly known for their sweet tooth – three or four teaspoons are the standard dose. The tea used is a type of jasmine tea that is a bit sweeter than standard black tea.

Java is famous for its coffee, as coffee beans grow very well there. You will hear the phrase 'a cup of java' used all over the world as slang for coffee. However, take care when asking for white coffee! Instead of standard milk, thick sweetened condensed milk is used, a Dutch habit that Indonesians adopted in the colonial days. You can always check whether you are asking for white coffee by asking 'susu kental atau susu biasa?'.
