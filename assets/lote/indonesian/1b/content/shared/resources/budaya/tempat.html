One of the best known cuisines in Indonesia is Padang food. You can find Rumah Makan Padang all over Indonesia. As well as the highly spicy food, RM Padang are known for their unique dining style. You can spot a Rumah Makan Padang by the piles of dishes that are stacked up in the window, usually in white metal bowls. Many are buka 24 jam. You don't order from a menu at the table â€“ a waiter will come over with anywhere up to ten dishes stacked on each arm and place the equivalent of every dish on the menu in front of you, along with white rice and water. You eat what you like the look of, and at the end the waiter counts up the number of dishes you have used.

Padang restaurants are always halal. This means that no pork or any other food prohibited by Islam (including alcohol) is served. Fresh fruit juices are nearly always available wherever you go in Indonesia – and fresh means the fruit is juiced right after you order it.


Warung tenda

Reproduced with the
permission of Laura Lochore

Pada sore hari, the pavements in many areas of town begin to resemble miniature camp sites, with warung tenda setting up. There are generally wooden trestle tables with plastic stools, a kompor and wajan filled with oil. A large bucket of es is usually also there to cool drinks, and enormous pots of nasi putih. The limited menu is advertised on spanduk or the sides of the tents. If you didn't want to eat lele, ikan laut, ayam atau bebek goreng, you wouldn't go to this warung tenda. While warung tenda don't look the most hygienic places to eat, the food is invariably fresh, cooked right in front of you, and very cheap and tasty.


Kaki lima

Reproduced with the
permission of Laura Lochore

Kaki lima are mobile food stalls. They have a limited menu and specialise in a certain kind of food, for example sate Madura, or kue terang bulan.
