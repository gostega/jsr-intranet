Tawar-menawar

Reproduced with the
permission of Jim and
Robyn Nottingham

Bargaining is a very important social interaction in the markets of Indonesia. As well as getting a better bargain if you are willing to spend the time to argue the price down, it shows a willingness to talk to someone. It is like a game and it almost seems rude if you refuse to bargain. One of the standard rules of bargaining is that you do not ask the price of something that you are not actually willing to discuss the price of. If you do not intend to buy, you should not ask.

For more expensive items such as clothing, sellers will often ask double or triple the price they expect to get. The buyer will then offer half the price they expect to pay. Each side has a good laugh at the other's outrageous price, and then the seller comes down in price and the buyer goes up. The seller will often praise their goods to the sky, saying 'Mutunya bagus sekali', if the buyer does not get in first and suggest that 'Mutunya kurang baik. Ada yang lebih bagus di toko lain'. In tough bargaining situations, the buyer may actually walk away saying, 'Tidak apa-apa. Saya akan beli di tempat lain.' The seller will often call the buyer back, agreeing to their last price.

For cheaper items such as fruit and vegetables, only a small amount of bargaining is expected. You are more likely to agree to the first price, say 'dua ribu untuk satu biji', and then ask 'Bisa kurang sedikit kalau saya beli dua biji?'
