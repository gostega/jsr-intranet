Monas is the Monumen Nasional which was commissioned in the 1960s by Indonesia's first President, Soekarno. It is located in central Jakarta in Lapangan Merdeka.

Monas represents Indonesia's fight for freedom and its independence from the Dutch. The monument is 137 metres high and is topped by a flame which is coated in 137 kilograms of gold. You can take an elevator up to the viewing platform just under the flame (it's very windy up there â€“ beware of wearing a skirt!) to get a panoramic view of Jakarta.

Inside the base of the monument is a small museum designed to showcase Indonesian national history. There are three-dimensional dioramas showing parts of the nationalist struggle. You can also see the coat of arms, the Pancasila and the Declaration of Independence displayed in gold leaf on black marble walls.
