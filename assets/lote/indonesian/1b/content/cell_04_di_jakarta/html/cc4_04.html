Daniel and Trish are getting ready to attend the upacara pembukaan Tantangan Indonesia. They have remembered Ibu Indra's advice and dressed formally, which is lucky, because upacara tend to be very formal occasions. There are nearly always formal speeches (with microphones and loud speakers wherever possible), spanduk, elaborate papan bunga and, most importantly, food. Food is generally laid out banquet style, and there is often nasi tumpeng, a cone-shaped mountain of yellow rice used to celebrate special occasions. There is often some form of entertainment also, usually in the form of traditional dancing or music.

Listen to the opening of the welcome speech at the pembukaan Tantangan Indonesia.

Ibu-ibu, bapak-bapak, saudara-saudari sekalian yang saya hormati. Dengan senang hati saya menyambut para hadirin di upacara pembukaan Tantangan Indonesia ini. Atas nama panitia Tantangan Indonesia, saya mengucapkan selamat datang di Indonesia kepada semua kontestan dari luar negeri. Kami senang sekali menerima kontestan dari Amerika, Australia, Jerman, Afrika Selatan, Jepang dan Korea. Kami mengucapkan semoga sukses kepada semua kontestan. Kami berharap waktu Anda di Indonesia menyenangkan dan menyegarkan hati Anda dan kami mohon maaf kalau ada kekurangan apapun yang alami sewaktu ikut acara Tantangan Indonesia.

Ladies and gentlemen whom I respect, I am pleased to welcome you all to the opening ceremony of Tantangan Indonesia. On behalf of the board of Tantangan Indonesia, I welcome all the contestants from overseas to Indonesia. We are very happy to receive contestants from America, Australia, Germany, South Africa, Japan and Korea. We wish you all success. We hope your time in Indonesia is enjoyable and refreshing and we apologise if there are any inadequacies which you experience while you are here participating in Tantangan Indonesia.
