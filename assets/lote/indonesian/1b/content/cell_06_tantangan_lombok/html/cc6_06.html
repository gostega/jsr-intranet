Remember these? These words go before verbs and add to the meaning of the sentence.

seharusnya harus perlu Perlu can also be a verb.
For example: Anda seharusnya mencuci tangan dulu. For example: Anda harus memotong wortel. For example: Anda perlu tambah garam. For example: Anda perlu setengah kilo beras.

Remember, you don't know the people you are talking to. Therefore you need to use formal terms of address – saya/Anda.

You can also use the sort of formal opening used in speeches – 'Para permirsa yang saya hormati …'

Consider other ways to keep your tone polite. For example, it would be really rude to say 'Kamu harus …' You can use Anda.

There are two ways of giving instructions in cooking without using 'you'.

Often, you can start with the verb instead of saying 'you have to' – for example:

Olesi roti dengan mentega. Spread the bread with butter.

The other way of doing it is to rearrange the sentence so that the object (in this case, the bread) that is being 'verbed' comes before the verb (in this case, spread), for example:

Roti diolesi dengan mentega.

Ikan digoreng selama lima menit.

Sayuran dicuci sebelum dipotong dan dimasak.

These are called passive sentences and use the di- form of the verb.

Choose a fairly simple recipe you can prepare.

  • Film yourself making it and explaining the process in Indonesian. You could do this with a partner, demonstrating teaching someone how to do it. Revise the useful phrases if you are going to choose this option.

    or

You should:

  • introduce yourself
  • script your recipe in Indonesian first
  • use sequencing words such as yang pertama, yang kedua, kemudian and so on
  • highlight key words and try to memorise them (for example, 'heat', 'stir' and the names for different ingredients)
  • put your script away and have a practice run, watch or listen to it, and then choose some areas to improve.
