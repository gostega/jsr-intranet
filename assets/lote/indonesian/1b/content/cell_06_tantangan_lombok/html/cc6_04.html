-kan

You have seen -kan used a number of times. -kan has several functions.

One of the functions is to make a verb require an object after it.

This type of verb is called transitive. In English, 'fry' is usually a transitive verb, because it is grammatically incorrect to say 'I am going to fry' – you have to say 'I am going to fry something'. You can say 'I am going to cook'. Here 'cook' is intransitive (it doesn't always need an object following it).

Me-kan or di-kan verbs are common in Indonesian recipes. If you are doing something to something, often a -kan is required.

Remember, in spoken language the me- is often dropped. It is also common not to use me- when giving instructions, as in recipes.
