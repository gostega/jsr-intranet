Se-

You have already seen se- meaning 'a' or 'one' (for example, seorang).

In the tantangan above, the words seseorang and sesuatu are used.

Seseorang means 'someone' (that could be anyone).

Sesuatu means 'something'.

Classifiers

In shopping (and cooking), you need to be able to recognise and use classifiers. Classifiers are used more frequently in Indonesian than in English. (If you are not sure of what a classifier is, an English example is 'pieces', as in three pieces of paper.)

Indonesian has a number of classifiers. These usually have literal meanings as well as being classifiers. For example, buah means fruit but 'sebuah nanas' is 'a pineapple'.

Remember, se- is the prefix used for single units. The number of items is used if more than one (for example dua buah nanas).

Here are some common classifiers:

  • batang – classifier for long stick-like items
  • biji – classifier for small round things or seeds
  • buah – classifier for fruit and other inanimate objects
  • butir – classifier for round objects, eg dua butir telur, sebutir mangga
  • ekor – classifier for animals, eg seekor ayam
  • helai – classifier for sheets of paper, cloth or leaves (including clothes)
  • lembar – classifier for sheets of paper
  • potong – general classifier for pieces, eg dua potong kue (also used for items of clothing)
  • siung
  • tusuk – classifier for setusuk sate
