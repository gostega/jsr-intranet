Cek jawaban

You will have noticed that Budi gives more instructions to Trish and Daniel, whereas he uses the words minta, ingin, tolong and semoga (which are a lot softer and more respectful) for Ibu Indra due to her status as an older person, a university lecturer and a friend of his mother's. If you were writing to someone you did not know well, such as Ibu Indra, to ask a favour, you would avoid using words like jangan. It would be impolite to order her to do or not to do something. You could be even more polite by avoiding using silakan or saying Anda bisa, as they can also sound like orders. For example, instead of saying 'Anda bisa telepon saya di 0422 444 444', or 'telepon saya di …' you may reword it to avoid sounding bossy 'Nomor telepon saya …' or 'saya dapat dihubungi'
