Persiapan

After the initial excitement, Daniel and Trish start to realise how much they don't know about going to Indonesia, and how much they need to find out. Their first points of call are their Indonesian contacts in Perth.

In this section, you will watch the video of Daniel and Trish's interviews with Indonesians living in Perth. This will develop your skills in being able to understand advice given in Indonesian, as well as helping you understand politeness conventions in conversations. You will also become familiar with such conventions in emails.

This section focuses on conversations, which will help you learn some useful strategies for coping when you don't understand spoken language. You will become more confident asking questions about travelling in Indonesia (including the weather, what to take, and what is appropriate to wear in different areas of Indonesia) in both casual and formal contexts.

You will:

  • draft a list of questions about travelling in Indonesia
  • ask advice about travel and be able to give advice yourself
  • view videos of native speakers giving travel advice
  • read and respond to emails and SMS to make appointments and advise on travel
  • look at an interactive map containing information on religion/clothing/tourism in various areas of Indonesia
  • give advice regarding clothing choices for different places in Indonesia.

Print out the kartu kemajuan to track your progress as you go through this section of work. You should also save it in a folder on your computer.

It should take you approximately eight hours to complete this section.
