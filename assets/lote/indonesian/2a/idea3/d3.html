Question words

Question words are important in Indonesian. If you know what the question word means, you can tell what sort of answer you should give. Listening for other key words in the question can also help you to predict what is being asked, even if you don’t know the meaning of all the words.

Take for example, ‘Kamu biasanya berbelanja di mana?’  You know from ‘di mana’ that they are asking about a place. From the key word ‘berbelanja’, you can recognise the question as essentially asking ‘where do you shop?’ even if you don’t know ‘biasanya’.

The placement of questions in Indonesian can be quite flexible – either at the beginning or end of a sentence. Generally speaking, the use of the question word at the beginning is more formal.

Pronouns and register

In spoken Indonesian, the word for ‘you’ or ‘I’ is often dropped and replaced with a more neutral ‘nya’. (Remember, kamu/Anda indicate a relationship so, if you aren’t sure of the relationship status, it is better to use the name or title).

‘Siapa nama Anda?’ becomes ‘Namanya siapa?’
‘Berapa umur Anda?’ becomes ‘Umurnya berapa?’ / ‘Umur Diana berapa?’

If you are thinking this is difficult, don’t worry! Tidak apa-apa. If you can identify the question word and some key words, you can predict the meaning of the question, so answering it is easier.
In spoken language ‘nya’ means ‘his’, ‘her’, ‘your’ and ‘the’.
For example: Saya mau makan. Di mana sendoknya? (I want to eat. Where is the fork?)
