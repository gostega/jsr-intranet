Passive/object focus verb prefixes

In Indonesian, the prefixes used with verbs change according to whether the sentence is active or passive.

Active/Subject focus sentences use me-, ber-, or base-word verbs.

Passive/Object focus sentences either use di-verbs or no affix, depending on whether the subject (actor) in the sentence is first, second or third person.

If you have trouble remembering what first, second and third person means, think:
     ‘I’m first, you’re second, and everyone else comes third’.

In this exercise, determine whether the person doing the action is 1st/2nd (I/you) or 3rd (someone else) person. In column 1, select either 1st/2nd (if it’s I, me, we or you) or 3rd (if it is he, she, they or someone else).

Secondly, work out if the sentence is active/subject focus or passive/object focus.
In column 2, select either active or passive.
This will then show you what the correct affix is.
