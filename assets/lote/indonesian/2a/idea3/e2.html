There are a few questions below relating to the questions and responses in the interview. As the formal register is more appropriate for an interview with someone you don’t know, the questions have been written in formal Indonesian. Answer using formal language, and with full sentences, where possible.

Sombong literally translates as ‘conceited’, but the feel or meaning of the word doesn’t translate well. Someone who is sombong is someone who seems too proud to socialise with other people. For example, if you are walking down the street and someone calls out to you and you don’t reply, you may hear the comment ‘wah, sombong’. Or if someone invites you to stop and chat and you can’t, the impression is that you are sombong. Jam karet (rubber time) leaves plenty of time for conversation, in many contexts. An exception is at school or university. Students are expected to be at class on time, but if the lecturer is half an hour late, tidak apa-apa, jam karet.
