Bangsa means ‘nation’ or ‘nationality’, and kebangsaan, whilst sometimes translated as ‘nationality’, is better translated as ‘nationalism’. The word implies a feeling of national pride and belonging as a member of the national community.

‘Lagu kebangsaan’ means ‘national song’, but it is sometimes incorrectly translated as ‘national anthem’. In Australia, there is one national anthem, but not many other songs that you would describe as ‘national songs’ or ‘nationalistic songs’. Indonesia has the one official national anthem, Indonesia Raya, and any number of ‘nationalistic songs’. One of the first songs children typically learn at school is the ‘Garuda Pancasila’ song, about the national coat of arms and five basic principles which were stated by Soekarno when he declared Indonesia’s national independence on 17 August 1945. The five principles of the Pancasila are:

  • Belief in One Supreme God
  • Just and civilised humanity
  • Unity of Indonesia
  • Democracy
  • Social justice.

National sentiment is very high in Indonesia, and much has been done by the government to develop a spirit of nationalism. You will find a lot of national images and references to national icons, such as the flag, in popular culture. This includes magazines, advertisements, and songs such as Cokelat’s Bendera, which was written for a movie of the same name in 2002. You may find it interesting to view the karaoke video clip of this song. Search for it on the Internet.
