Bahasa gaul

One feature you may have noticed is the use of Bahasa gaul which is used as a kind of ‘code-language’ for teenagers. Bahasa gaul includes the following features.

  1. Slang words change with fashion, and quickly become out of date. (Can you think of some cool words or sayings that you used in primary school and don’t any more?)
  2. Words can be totally different in slang to their formal equivalent.
  3. Patterns of affixation (prefixes and suffixes) and abbreviation differ from formal language.
  4. ‘Ind-lish’ (a mixture of Indonesian and English) is used.
  5. Spellings are changed (like using ‘ur’ for ‘you are’ or ‘your’ in English SMS or email).
  6. Words are amalgamated (bits of words are put together).
