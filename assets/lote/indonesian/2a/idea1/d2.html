Gue/lu?

Different words are used for ‘I’ and ‘you’ according to the context of communication (the situation and the person you are addressing).

I
You
Context
saya Anda indicates formality, and/or that you do not know the person very well
aku kamu indicates an informal or close situation or relationship
gue lu indicates a very informal or close situation or relationship

Names or titles can also be used as ‘I’ or ‘you’. This can indicate closeness (if a name) or formality (if a title).

Kau from engkau is also used for ‘you’ and can indicate a very close relationship and a great degree of respect. For example, Christians refer to God as Engkau or Kau in prayer.

By listening to the forms of ‘I’ and ‘you’ used in conversations, you can work out something about the relationship between the speakers.
