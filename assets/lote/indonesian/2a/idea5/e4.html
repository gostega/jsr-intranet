ongkos – price (service only, not goods/objects)

Ongkos and harga both mean ‘price'. Technically, harga is used for the price of goods, and ongkos refers to the price of services (eg, berapa ongkos tiket bis malam?). However, as you will see in the interview, harga is now more commonly and broadly used in everyday speech.

Many Chinese Indonesians are Protestant Christian (Kristen) or Catholic (Katolik). In the 1960s, there were strong political tensions between the Communist Party (Partai Komunis Indonesia) and the army. At that time, the country was led by Soekarno, the first president of Indonesia. On 30 September 1965, seven right-wing anti-communist army generals were kidnapped and murdered, supposedly by the communist party, in an attempted coup. An army faction led by Major General Soeharto thwarted the coup attempt, which resulted in Soeharto becoming the second president of Indonesia.

After the events of 30 September, there were widespread massacres of suspected communists. Due to the link between China and communism, and the general unpopularity of Chinese due to their success in business and marginalisation as an ethnic minority by both the Dutch and the Soekarno government, many Chinese were accused of being communists and attacked. In order to avoid persecution, many Chinese converted to Protestant Christianity or Catholicism and Indonesianised their names at this time.
