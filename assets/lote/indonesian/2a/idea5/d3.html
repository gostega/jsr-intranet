Nouns: ke-an/-an

In Indonesian, a word ending in -an will nearly always be a noun. Ke-an words are also nouns. There is no change to the pattern of the base-word with the ke-an affixation.

pandai

clever

kepandaian

cleverness

cepat

fast

kecepatan

speed

indah

beautiful

keindahan  

beauty (for views/scenery)

senang

happy

kesenangan

happiness/hobby

harus

have to

keharusan

obligation/requirement

berangkat

leave

keberangkatan

departure

datang

arrive

kedatangan

arrival

In some cases, ke-an indicates that you are ‘negatively affected by (the base-word)', or ‘too (the base-word)'.

hujan

rain

kehujanan

caught in the rain

panas

hot

kepanasan

too hot

tinggal

live

ketinggalan

left behind (by)

siang

middle of the day

kesiangan

overslept

banjir

flood

kebanjiran

affected by floods / inundated

celaka

misfortune

kecelakaan

accident

macet

traffic jam

kemacetan

caught in traffic

haus

thirsty

kehausan

dehydrated

lapar

hungry

kelaparan

starving
