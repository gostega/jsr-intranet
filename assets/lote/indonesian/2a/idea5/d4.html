Tanda-tanda

Tanda-tanda artinya signs. When you are travelling, you will see a lot of signs with instructions on them: do this, don't do that. It is important to recognise key instruction words and whether they are telling you to do something or not to do something.

Do this!

-lah is a suffix used at the end of verbs to make the word a polite instruction. (Remember, ‘!' is used in Indonesian to indicate an instruction, not to indicate excitement!).

If you are giving directions, you would be likely to use ‘-lah'.

Ikutilah jalan ini sampai perempatan, lalu belok kiri. Masuklah jalur kiri, lalu belok kiri lagi. Ada restoran tidak jauh dari pojok itu.

Harap is telling you that you are expected to do something. It is polite, but more like your teacher telling you to ‘please hand in your homework' than ‘please can I get a drink' - it is definitely more an order than a request, and usually used by those with authority to give that order.

 

Mohon is a very polite request.


Don't do this!

Jangan! Jangan is telling you definitely not to do something.

Dilarang is forbidden/prohibited.

Awas is beware!

Hati-hati is be careful!

Lain-lain

Other useful sign words include:

Bahaya - danger

Perhatian – attention

Khusus – especially for.
For example, parkir khusus pegawai.

Umum – public
