Strategi membaca

Cari pengertian!

The most important things to remember when dealing with an unfamiliar reading text are:

  • you don’t have to understand every word
  • don’t worry, you can do it!

Pre-reading

Before you get seriously into reading a text, you should use some simple strategies to gain clues as to what the text is about. For example:

  • Recognise clues that tell you what kind of text it is (eg magazine, newspaper article, email).
  • Use all the visual and contextual clues you can (pictures, layout, headings) to get a general idea of themes, purpose and main topic of the text.
  • Look at titles and subtitles for information.

Reading

When reading you can use a range of strategies, including the following.

  • Read the headings and topic sentences (first sentence) of each paragraph.
  • Use highlighting to either identify words you do know, or words you don’t know but think are important to deciphering the meaning.
  • Skim-read to get a general impression of the text, ignoring words you don’t know.
  • Use key words and cognates (words that are adapted from English) to deduce meaning. Guess the meaning of words from the words around them.
  • Look at base-words to guess the meaning of the word, and use your knowledge of affixes (for example, it starts with a me-, therefore it’s a verb or doing word) to make meaning.
  • Don’t overuse your dictionary! Only look up words that seem crucial and whose meaning you can’t guess.

Reading strategy practice

  1. Choose a passage to read without referring to your dictionary.
  2. Take a minute to write down what you think the passage is about.
  3. Now underline or highlight words you don’t know and think are significant.
  4. Pick five of these words which you think are most important to understand more of the passage and underline them. Look them up now.
  5. Read the passage again. Were the five words useful in working out more of the meaning?
  6. Look up some other words. Were any of them more important?
You do not need to know every word to get the gist of a passage.
Sometimes you can work out answers to questions without looking up any words. You can do this by scanning the text for specific information you need to complete the task.
