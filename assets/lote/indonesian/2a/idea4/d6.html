Conjunctions

Conjunctions are linking words which can be used to extend your writing. You are already familiar with some conjunctions like 'karena'. Adding to the range of conjunctions you can use helps to make your writing more sophisticated.

You already saw sehingga used in the last exercise.

  • Musik itu begitu mengharukan sehingga beberapa orang menangis.
  • Cerita film itu sangat membingungkan sehingga saya tidak bisa konsentrasi lagi.

Sehingga means 'so’ or 'to the point that’ and indicates a non-intentional action.
Supaya can also mean 'so’ or 'in order to’ but indicates deliberate intention. For example:

  • Saya mengerjakan PR supaya bisa mendapat nilai yang bagus.

Agar is similar, meaning 'so that’ with a deliberate action in mind.

  • Saya belajar agar bisa mendapat nilai tinggi.

Akibatnya means 'as a consequence’ or 'the result of that action’.

  • Saya bangun terlambat, akibatnya saya ketinggalan bis.
  • Kalau manusia tidak mempedulikan lingkungan alam, akibatnya akan buruk.

Jadi is a more general form of 'so’, and can be used at the start of a phrase in speech, like 'so, what shall we do next?’ or in the middle of a sentence. It is one of the most commonly heard conjunctions used in speech.

  • Ari lupa mengerjakan tugasnya di rumah, jadi dia kena marah ibunya.
