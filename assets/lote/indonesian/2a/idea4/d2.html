Under Soeharto’s New Order government, which was in power from 1965–1998, there were strict censorship laws in Indonesia. These made sure that views contrary to the government were not publicised. People who broke these laws faced the threat of jail terms, or worse. In publicly-screened films, even a chaste kiss between two fully clothed adults was blacked out as dangerous to society’s morals. Not long after the fall of the Soeharto government in May 1998, the Departemen Penerangan (Department of Information, which was in charge of censorship) was abolished, and the film industry began to flourish. It took another four years before Indonesia’s first on-screen kiss happened in Ada Apa Dengan Cinta? in 2002. The song Bento about Soeharto’s son Tommy’s controversial business dealings, which had landed its creator (Iwan Fals) in jail, was even featured as an example of a lagu bangsa in the second season of Indonesian Idol, showing just how much things have changed.
