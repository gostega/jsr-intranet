Affixes

In Indonesian, a range of affixes can be used with base-words to modify the meaning of the word. For example:

  • Pe- often indicates a person:
    • who does the base-word; for example, pemenang = winner
    • someone with the characteristics of the base-word; for example, pemalu = a shy person.
  • Pe- sometimes indicates an object or tool for doing the base-word; for example, pemotong = a cutting implement such as a guillotine.
  • Me-/di-/ber- indicates a verb.
  • -an indicates a noun or object.

Recognising what the affix does helps you to make meaning of the word. Fill out the table on the worksheet attached to familiarise yourself with some of the words you will find in this unit.

CD atau CD?

You need to be careful when pronouncing CD in Indonesian. As with other acronyms taken from English, like AC for air conditioner, the letters are pronounced more closely to the English pronunciation than to the Indonesian letter pronunciation. So CD is said like ‘seh-deh’ rather than the Indonesian ‘cheh-deh’. Going into a music store and using the Indonesian pronunciation to ask for a ‘che-deh’ is likely to result in some giggles, as you are actually asking for ‘celana dalam’ which means ‘underpants’.
