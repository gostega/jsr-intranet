Describing with 'me-kan’

A number of adjectives such as senang, sedih, terkejut ... can be used to describe feelings or emotional states. Films, music or other bentuk kesenian often cause us to experience certain emotions.

In Indonesian, the circumfix (prefix + suffix) me-kan is often causative. This means that it causes the state of being whatever the base-word is.

For example:

senang

happy

menyenangkan

causes happiness (enjoyable)

sedih

sad

menyedihkan

saddening

terkejut

surprised

mengejutkan

surprising

heran

amazed

mengherankan

amazing

haru

touched

mengharukan

touching

kesan

impression

mengesankan

impressive

jengkel

annoyed

menjengkelkan

annoying

jijik

disgusting/creepy

menjijikkan

makes you feel disgusted/creepy

bingung

confused

membingungkan

confusing

Me-kan verbs often need an object after them, except where the person being acted on is obvious. For example, if I say, 'film itu sangat mengherankan’, it is obvious that the person being amazed is me, therefore it is unnecessary to say 'film itu mengherankan saya’.
