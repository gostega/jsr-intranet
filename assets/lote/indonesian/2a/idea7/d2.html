Comparisons

There are two very simple ways of comparing things in Indonesian. One way is to say that something is as adjective (good/hot/funny) as something else. A second way is to say that something is more adjective than something else (for example, better than, hotter than, funnier than and so on).

You can also express preference using ‘lebih … daripada’ by saying ‘lebih suka ... daripada’. For example:
            Saya lebih suka masakan Indonesia daripada masakan Eropa.

Dibandingkan dengan ...

If you want to sound a little more sophisticated, rather than saying ‘lebih ... daripada’, you can say ‘dibandingkan dengan’. For example, the following sentences all mean the same thing.

Nasi uduk lebih enak daripada nasi kuning.
Nasi uduk lebih enak, kalau/jika dibandingkan dengan nasi kuning.

Kalau/Jika dibandingkan dengan nasi kuning, nasi uduk yang lebih enak.

Dibandingkan means ‘compared with’. As a di- verb it is passive.
Kalau dibandingkan dengan Australia, Indonesia...
Membandingkan means ‘to compare with’ (active sentence).
Kalau membandingkan Australia dengan Indonesia...


Hati-hati!

In Indonesian, you always use dengan to compare things. Never use  ke/kepada in comparisons.
