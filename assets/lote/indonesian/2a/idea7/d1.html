Periklanan

Advertising is extremely important to magazines as a source of funds, either directly (through the placement of advertisements) or indirectly (through providing clothes for fashion features).

Think of at least three features (or textual conventions) of advertisements. Features may relate to the type of language used, or layout and formatting.

Some features include (but are not limited to):

  • use of persuasive language
  • use of adjectives (describing words) and superlatives (the biggest, best, newest, freshest.)
  • image of product
  • brand name in large font

In addition to these general features, linguistic features of Indonesian advertisements include:

  • the use of imperatives such as -lah
  • the use of English and cognates (Indonesian words adapted from English) such as diskon and persen
  • the use of comparative words and phrases such as makin (the more) and lebih … daripada (more … than)
  • the use of superlatives such as ter- (the most)
  • the use of (me)-kan verbs meaning ‘to make’, such as menghaluskan.

As well as advertisements for the usual clothing, cosmetics, and hygiene products, cigarettes are heavily advertised in Indonesian magazines aimed at men or boys. According to the World Health Organisation, ‘About 69.1% of Indonesian men 20 years or older smoke regularly, with higher rates in rural areas (74.0%)’ (World Health Organisation, 2002).  Women are not encouraged to smoke as it is perceived to pose a risk to their health that may affect their child-bearing ability. Cigarette companies sponsor sporting and cultural events such as rock concerts, and also sponsor sections of magazines, such as the Resensi section, music charts, and so on. Popular television shows, particularly those appealing to the teenage demographic, are also sponsored by tobacco companies. It is not uncommon to see the logo of a popular brand of cigarettes remain in the top corner of the screen for the entire program.

Persuasive language

Descriptive language is one indicator of good writing. The next step in sophistication is the ability to use persuasive language. Advertisements are good, simple examples of texts that use persuasive language.

Brainstorm a list of the types of words (such as adjectives), phrases and techniques commonly seen in advertisements that help persuade people to buy the product.

Some suggestions include:

Adjectives – New! Clean!

Imperatives – Buy now! For example, -lah

Incentives – Buy one get one free. For example, Diskon!

Inclusivity – so you aren’t left out. Inclusive language – the advertisement treats you as a friend. For example, Ayo dong!

You probably thought of many more examples than the answers given. The more you can think of now, the greater your ability in working with this kind of text type will be.

You may also hear the terms Bunda (Ibu) and Ayah (Bapak) used as highly respectful terms of address for parents.

Direct address

Advertisements often address the reader directly by asking questions or issuing an invitation. This engages the reader’s interest and persuades them to purchase the product. For example:

       Ayo dong!

       Yuk, ikut!

These also demonstrate the use of inclusive language – writing to include the reader. You will also see words like kita and bareng/bersama used in advertisements.

Another way of engaging the reader is asking questions, which are often indicated by the  -kah suffix. For example

       Perlukah Anda ...

Imperatives and suggestions

An imperative is a command. You already know that the -lah suffix makes a word an imperative in Indonesian, and that the exclamation sign (!) also indicates a required action. You will see words with -lah used in advertisements, telling the consumer to buy this product, use that brand, and so on. For example:

       Belilah Baodan Pemutih Kulit baru!

       Gunakanlah Cuci-o setiap hari!

Other words such as perlu, mesti, and harus also make statements seem more imperative in advertisements. You have also seen the word jangan used as a negative imperative.

Dropping the me-

Me-kan verbs are often used to describe the effect of a product. This makes sense if you remember that one of the functions of me-kan is causative. In advertisements for beauty products, for example, the effects of the product are emphasised by the use of me-kan verbs.

Produk ini membersihkan, menjernihkan dan melembabkan kulit.
