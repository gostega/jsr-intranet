This topic focuses on sopan santun bertelepon. You will find out the ways to open and close phone conversations in Indonesian, and how to speak politely to different groups of people.

When you are making a phone call in your first language, there are certain conventions (ways of speaking, ways of opening and closing the conversation, things that are considered polite or impolite) that you follow without even thinking about, for example, in English it is polite to say, ‘Hello, this is … may I please speak to …?’.

These conventions (known as textual conventions) differ from culture to culture. In Indonesian, the sort of language you use changes depending on the person with whom you are speaking, for example, a friend, your boss, or someone you don’t know. It is important to be aware of the conventions that are appropriate to the situation in order to communicate politely and appropriately.

Outcomes

  • You will become aware of conventions of phone calls in Indonesia.
  • You will be able to choose the right register for a conversation.
  • You will be able to hold a phone conversation using the appropriate language and conventions.
