Tugas 5

Many teen magazines use an extreme amount of bahasa gaul. Hai magazine deliberately targets its 15–17-year-old (predominantly male) audience by using the kind of language used by its readers, rather than bahasa Indonesia yang baik dan benar.

You may find it useful to review slang in Pojok bahasa before attempting this tugas.

Even though it is unlikely that you will be able to understand it all, you should be able to find:

  • some standard Indonesian words that you recognise
  • some English mixed through
  • some Indonesian words that have modified spelling such as:
    • the use of ‘e’, for example, benar/bener; teman/temen; dapat/dapet
    • ‘lazy’ letter drop spelling, for example, asyik/asik, saja/aja
    • The use of ‘-in’ instead of ‘-kan’ as a suffix
  • some common slang words (for example, nggak, kok, banget).

Using this knowledge and these strategies, you will be able to get some impression of the content of this page.
