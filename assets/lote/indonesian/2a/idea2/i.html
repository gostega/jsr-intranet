Ramalan, perbintangan or horoskop seem to be an essential part of many magazines! Whether people believe in them, or consider them as an amusing way to meluangkan waktu, horoscopes and predictions are popular in Indonesia.

In Indonesia, different groups have different beliefs relating to religion, ethnic group traditions or local superstitions. They are often a mixture of animistic and religious beliefs. For example, if you bathe in a lake during a full moon, your face and body will shine and you will be loved by all.  Or if your dog scratches segala macam barang, someone in your family will receive some unexpected good fortune. Such beliefs are reflected in popular culture, with TV shows and films about the supernatural abounding.

Usually in magazines, the standard 12 star-sign horoscope is included; however, indigenous and other fortune-telling techniques also make appearances in the national media. Since the end of Soeharto’s New Order regime in 1998, under which overt representations of Chinese culture were banned (including the celebration of Chinese New Year), Chinese fortune-telling traditions such as the Year of the Animal birth signs and Feng Shui have become very popular.
