Attività 3 Scioperi, treni fermi per otto ore

Scioperi

In recent times, train, bus and air strikes have a common feature in Italy. The strikes are to do with workplace laws that Italian people do not favour.

It’s a good idea to find out before using public transport whether a sciopero has been scheduled for the day. Information about train services during a strike can be found at www.trenitalia.com.
Search for: Servizi essenziali in caso di sciopero.

How will the scheduled train strike impact on the following travellers who are relying on train transport that day?

  1. Carolina goes to a liceo a Roma. She lives on the outskirts of Rome and to get to school on time she needs to leave home at 7 am. Classes finish at 1 pm.


    She will be able to get to school but she will not be able to catch a train home until 5 pm when the strike ends.

  2. Chiara and Marta are going to Parigi for a week. Their flight leaves from Fiumicino airport. They need to catch a train to get to the airport.


    The train service to the airport will not be affected. If there is a problem there will be a bus service provided.

  3. Maurizio works in a bank and travels to work on the train. He begins work at 8.30 am and finishes at 5 pm.


    The strike will not affect him as the strike is between 9 am and 5 pm.

  4. Claudio works in a supermarket and gets to work by train. Usually he begins work at 9 am. On the day of the strike, supermarkets are closed in the morning and don’t open until 12.


    The strike begins at 9 am. So he will have to find an alternative means of transport or get to his work site before the strike begins.

  5. Il signor Bellini has a specialist’s appointment at the hospital at 10 am. He is reliant on the train to get to his appointment.


    He will need to get to his destination before 9 am. He will have a bit of a wait. The problem, however, is getting home. Trains do not resume service until 5 pm.
