Italian trains

The railway system is serviced by several types of trains. From the locale, which is found in more remote areas and which stops at every station, to the EuroStar, a fast train that travels between different capital cities in Europe.

When looking at an Italian train timetable, you will notice the following abbreviations:

ES – EuroStar: a fast comfortable service that travels between European countries.

EC – Eurocity: an international express train with very few stops.

IC – Intercity: an express train that stops only at major stations (stops at main stations: supplement).

R – Rapido: long-distance express that stops in most major cities; high-speed intercity (reservations compulsory).

EXP – Espresso: a long-distance train that stops at the main stations.

D – Diretto: a much slower train that stops at many local stations.

L – Locale: a slow train which stops at every station on the itinerary.

Some fast trains require a supplemento (extra fare on top of the normal cost because it is a faster train) to be paid.

Some trains only run on working days (feriale); holidays/Sundays (festivo) or Saturdays; and the eve of a holiday (pre-festivo).

È importante essere in regola con il biglietto. You must convalidare il biglietto at the macchinette obliteratrici before you get on board. Depending on the size of the station, these are situated in various locations – within the stazione itself, in the underpass and on i binari.

The penalty for failing to validate your ticket is a multa if you get caught.

If you have failed to validate your ticket (for whatever reason), you should report this to the Capotreno once you board the train. You will be required to pay a small difference; however, you will avoid a multa of around € 20,45.
