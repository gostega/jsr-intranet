Attività 1 Arrivo all’aeroporto

On arrival at an Italian airport it is possible to clear customs without having your passport checked or stamped. In reality, it may be possible that you go through the airport without even having to speak any Italian! All’aeroporto most of the signs and announcements are usually both in Italian and English and sometimes in the language of the destination or origin of the flights.

Here are some expressions that will be useful all’aeroporto.

All’aeroporto: Frasi utili  
Scusi, dov’è il check-in per il volo QF672 per Singapore? Where do I check in for flight QF672 to Singapore?
Scusi, qual è l’uscita per il volo per Milano? Which is the departure gate for the flight to Milan?
Scusi, dov’è il bagaglio del volo da Londra? Where is the luggage for the flight from London?
Il volo AZ123 per Parigi è in ritardo di venticinque minuti. Flight AZ123 to Paris is delayed by 25 minutes.

What if you are not sure which way to go to get out?

What if you need to get to another airport terminal?

What if you want to know how to get transport into the city?

What if your luggage is damaged or, worse still, does not arrive?

In these situations, knowledge of Italian will help you cope with the unexpected!

Which phrases can you use or modify in the conversation Assistenza bagagli.


Make a note of the questions and phrases that you require to find out information about leaving your luggage at the airport.

The formal/polite (Lei) form of address is used in the conversation.

Look at the following examples used by l’impiegata:

  1. Buongiorno, mi dica.
  2. Con quale volo è arrivato a Fiumicino?
  3. Ha i dettagli del bagaglio?

The third person singular form of the verb is used when speaking to one person.

The polite form of address will reoccur often throughout this section.
