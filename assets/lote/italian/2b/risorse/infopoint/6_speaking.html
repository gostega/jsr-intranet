Speaking strategies

Tips for speaking

If you don't know how to say a word, try and explain it in another way using other words. For example:

una cosa per …
un tipo di …
There are many ways of expressing the same idea.

You cannot express what you want to say in Italian using the same structures you use in English! Try and keep your structures in Italian clear and simple.

Revise regularly and try to incorporate some of the words, phrases and idiomatic expressions into your responses.

Prepare well for a specific speaking task. Prepare answers to questions that are likely to be asked. However, you need to be flexible. The questions asked may be different to what you anticipated and you need to adapt the information.

If you have access to other students of Italian, Italian exchange students and/or people in the local Italian community take every opportunity to speak Italian as much as possible.

The interview

Listen for question words as they tell you something about the response.

Ensure your responses are appropriate to the questions asked.

If you do not understand ask for clarification in Italian. For example:

Prego? Non ho capito.

Where possible, extend and give detail in your answers.

Don't worry if you make mistakes. If you do realise you can correct yourself.

Use the appropriate form of address for the situation:

Lei – formal situation
Tu – informal situation


Sustaining verbal communication

Disclaimers

Mi dispiace non capisco bene l’italiano. I’m sorry, I don’t understand Italian well.
Mi dispiace, non parlo italiano molto bene. I’m sorry, I don’t speak Italian very well.
Scusi se faccio molti sbagli. Excuse me if I make a lot of mistakes.
Mi dispiace, sono molto nervoso/a. I’m sorry, I’m very nervous.

Repair strategies

If you don’t understand a question or any information, don’t be afraid to ask for help and for clarification.

Può parlare più piano per piacere? Can you please speak slower?
Prego? Non capisco la domanda. I beg your pardon? I don’t understand the question.
Può ripetere la domanda per piacere? Can you repeat the question, please?
Cosa vuole dire ... ? What is the meaning of ... ?
Non capisco la frase ... Cosa vuole dire? I don’t understand the phrase ... What does it mean?
Umm, ho dimenticato la parola per ... Umm, I’ve forgotten the word for ...

Vuoi dire ... ?   (informal)

Vuole dire ... ?  (formal)		 Do you mean ... ?
Cosa vuole dire? What does it mean?


If you are in Italy, most native speakers are more than happy to help you out if they can. Not everyone has a knowledge of English, so you need to be able to ask for help in Italian.
