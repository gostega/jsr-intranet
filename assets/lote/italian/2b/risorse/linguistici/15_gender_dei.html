Il genere dei sostantivi – Il singolare

In English, nouns are neither masculine nor feminine. We do not say that a computer is feminine or that a chair is masculine.

In Italian, however, all nouns have a gender. They are either maschile or femminile.
The gender of a noun is indicated by its ending or from the definite or indefinite article (the words 'the' and 'a/an') placed before it.

  • Masculine nouns usually end in –o.
  • Feminine nouns usually end in –a.
  • Some masculine and feminine nouns end in –e.

Look at the following sentences. The gender of the nouns is shown by the articles that are placed before them.

Maschile Femminile
Il motorino è nuovo.
The scooter is new.		 La vita è più bella con l’iPod®.
Life is more beautiful with an iPod®.
Il gelato è un cibo fantastico.
Ice-cream is a fantastic food.		 La musica italiana è bella.
Italian music is beautiful.
Il telefonino è molto utile.
The mobile phone is very useful.		 La famiglia è molto importante in Italia.
The family is very important in Italy.
Maschile o femminile

Italian nouns which end in –e can be either masculine or feminine.

The dictionary will help you with this. After a dictionary entry, an italic f indicates a feminine word and an m, a masculine word.

  • Io amo navigare la rete (f). – I love to surf the Internet.
  • Il cellulare (m) è molto utile. – The mobile phone is very useful.

 

Il genere dei sostantivi – Il plurale

Nouns, as well as having a gender, can be either singular (when they refer to one thing) or plural (when they refer to more than one thing). You have already learnt about the singular of nouns. Now we will look at the plurals of nouns.

Masculine and feminine nouns that end in –e in the singular change to –i in the plural.

The dictionary will help you with this. After a dictionary entry, an italic f indicates a feminine word and an m a masculine word.

studente studenti (m)

There are other exceptions to these rules.

Sometimes the abbreviation inv (invariable) will be used. This indicates a noun that has the same form in the singular and the plural.
