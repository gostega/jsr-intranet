I pronomi indiretti

Indirect object pronouns answer the question ‘to whom’ or ‘for whom’? Generally in English the word ‘to’ is omitted.

I pronomi indiretti are as follows:

Singolare    
mi to/for me

(Tu) mi dai il passaporto.

You give me (to me) the passport.
ti to/for you

(Lui) ti domanda se vai al Colosseo.

He asks you if you are going to the Colosseum.
gli to/for him, to it (masculine)

(Io) gli regalo una foto di Firenze.

I give him (to him) a photo of Florence.
le to/for her to it (feminine)

Loro le scrivono un’ e-cartolina dall’Italia.

They write an e-postcard to her from Italy.
Le to/for you (polite)

Signora, Le telefoniamo riguardo la prenotazione.

Madam, we will call you regarding the booking.

 

Plurale    
ci to/for us

(Voi) ci insegnate ad andare in motorino.

You teach us (to us) how to ride a scooter.
vi to/for you (all)

(Io) vi preparo un piatto di spaghetti.

I prepare for you a plate of spaghetti.
loro to/for them

Mostriamo loro i monumenti più famosi.

We show to them the most famous monuments.
*gli to/for them

Gli mostriamo i monumenti più famosi.

We show to them the most famous monuments.
Loro to/for you (all, polite)

Signori, domando Loro di non fumare sul treno.

Gentlemen, I ask you not to smoke on the train.
  1. The indirect object is the receiver of the action – ‘who’ or ‘what’ the action is being directed towards.
  2. Indirect object pronouns are identical in form to direct object pronouns, except for the third person forms gli, le, and loro.

  3. Indirect object pronouns go before a verb except for loro, which will always follow the verb.

    Per esempio:

    Marisa dà la fotografia a Luigi. –> Marisa gli dà la fotografia.

    * In spoken Italian, gli is often used instead of loro to replace ‘to/for them’.


L’uso di ne, ci and vi

Ne, ci and vi are pronouns that replace a noun that has been already mentioned in a phrase.
Look at the following examples.

I pronomi ne, ci, vi

ne

from there, of it, of them


It is always necessary to use
ne when replacing numbers
and adjectives of quantity.

Sono tornata dall’Italia oggi.
Ne sono tornata oggi.
I have come back from there today.

Noi prendiamo un chilo di banane.
Ne prendiamo un chilo.

We will have a kilo of them.
ci there, in it, to it

Noi andiamo a Venezia sabato mattina.
Noi ci andiamo sabato mattina.

We are going there Saturday morning.
vi there, in, to it

Loro vengono ad Assisi domani.
Loro vi vengono domani.

They are coming there tomorrow.

 

There is no difference between ci and vi, although ci is more common nowadays. You may see vi used more commonly in written Italian.
