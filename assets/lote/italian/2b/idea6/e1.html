L’Italia in Australia

Attività 1 Alla scoperta dell’Italia nella tua città!

In this attività you will have the opportunity to explore the Italian presence in your local area with an emphasis on events, festivals and entertainment.

The Italian contribution to the Australian lifestyle is highlighted in the following excerpts. The final one succinctly captures Australia’s relationship with Italy today and illustrates that the Italian presence in Australia is still a very important component of our society.

Italians today enjoy the status of being senior partners in multicultural Australia. Defined as the largest and one of the oldest non-English speaking immigrant groups, they are held up as examples of successful integration. It would be difficult to imagine an Australia without the markers of their presence, including the ubiquitous cappuccino machine, alfresco cafés and Italian restaurants.

Excerpt from A Brief History of Italians in Western Australia (8 November 2004), reproduced with the permission of Loretta Baldassar.


Even people who aren’t aware of the role Italians played in the early days of our construction, engineering, mining, timber and fishing industries, appreciate that they’d be lost without cappuccino machines, alfresco dining and the pasta dishes we now take for granted as part of our healthy, Mediterranean diets ...

... And although we have far fewer Italians migrating to Western Australia these days, researchers tell us that the relationship with Italy is being cemented by cultural, education and economic exchange:

  • family links
  • visits and tourism
  • dual citizenship
  • and a new population category – Italian Australians.

(The Hon. Alan Carpenter MLA, to the Western Australian Italian Club Business Forum).

Taken from an address by The Hon. Alan Carpenter, MLA, Premier of Western Australia, to the Western Australian Italian Club Business Forum on 24 March 2006 and reproduced with the permission of the Office of the Premier.


If you would like to read the whole speech, you can find it at http://www.premier.wa.gov.au, select Speeches >> Archive/2006.

Una guida: Alla scoperta dell’Italia nella tua città!

To begin your research, visit the websites of the following Italian bodies/organisations in your city and look for events and promotions.

Go to google.com.au (or another search engine of your choice) to find the web addresses.

You will also find further links on these sites that will help you in your research:

  • Italian Consulate
  • ICCI – Italian Chamber of Commerce & Industry
  • Italo-Australian Welfare & Cultural Centre
  • Local Italian and regional Clubs
  • Comites (Com.It.Es – Comitato degli Italiani all’Estero).

The following bodies may also have useful information:

  • Local government
  • Dante Alighieri Society
  • Opera company/theatre in your city
  • Palace Cinemas
  • Slow Food
  • SBS TV and radio, local/community radio, http://www.italianmedia.com.au.

Some ideas as to what events may be available:

  • Festa della Repubblica
  • Italian film festival
  • Opera
  • Theatre
  • Music/concerts
  • Exhibitions
  • Slow Food events
  • Wine/food tasting
  • Italian day at the races
  • Festivals
  • Carnivals
  • Fashion shows
  • Sporting events/competitions.

Courses/workshops:

  • Olive preserving, tomato sauce-making, sausage-making, among others
  • Cooking classes.
