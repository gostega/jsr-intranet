Il possessivo

The possessive adjectives (eg my, your, his) are used to express ownership or relationship. They:

  • agree in gender and number with the noun that follows
  • agree with the object possessed
  • do not agree with the person or thing that possesses the noun – it makes no difference whether the owner is male or female.

The definite article is generally used before possessive adjectives.

Il possessivo with family members

The rules for the use or omission of the definite article with possessive adjectives are as follows.

You don't use the definite article with singular members of the family.

Mio padre si chiama Carlo e mia madre si chiama Diana.
However, the definite article is always used with the possessive loro.

Il loro padre si chiama Carlo.
Include the definite article if the noun indicating the family member is qualified by an adjective or is modified in any way.

il mio fratello più grande

la mia sorellina*

*The – ino/a ending acts as a diminutive. It indicates smallness, cuteness.
The definite article is always used before a plural noun referring to family members, whether modified or not.

i miei genitori

i miei fratellini

If you use the forms mamma and papà you should use the definite article with the possessive. For example:

La mia mamma è italiana.

Many Italians, however, will omit the article when speaking.

Use of 'di' to show possession

The preposition di can also be used to indicate possession.
la ragazza di mio fratello
ll compleanno di mia madre

The possessor (Marco and Giorgia) in the following examples goes after the di.
la sorella di Marco.
il ragazzo di Giorgia.
