Aggettivi

Aggettivi are describing words. In Italian an adjective usually follows the noun (thing or person) it is describing.

In English the adjective is usually placed before the noun. For example:

Milan is a lively city. – Milano è una città movimentata.

Buono e grande

The adjective buono usually goes before the noun. When it is in this position it changes and follows the pattern of the indefinite article.

Before a masc word beginning with a vowel or a consonant:

un uomo un buon uomo a good man

Before a masc word beginning with a 'z' or a 's' + consonant:

uno zaino
uno studente		 un buono zaino
un buono studente		 a good backpack
a good student

When the noun is feminine and starts with a consonant:

una donna una buona donna a good woman

Before a fem noun starting with a vowel:

un'amica una buon'amica a good friend (f)

The adjective grande can go before or after a noun. Often context will determine the position grande will take.
For example:

Un grand'uomo means 'a great/important man', but
un uomo grande means 'a large man'.

If grande goes after the noun, it has two forms – grande for singular nouns and grandi if the noun is plural.

Before a noun, however, grande has several forms.

Un grand'uomo
Un grande scultore
Una grand'attrice		 Un gran palazzo
Una grande storia d'amore
