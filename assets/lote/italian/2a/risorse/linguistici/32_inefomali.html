Pronomi informali e formali

Italian has two levels of speech:

  • The familiar or informal level is used with children, relatives and friends. This is the form that young people use when talking amongst themselves.
  • The polite or formal level is used in business and other formal situations (ie at the bank, in shops) and young people talking to adults who they do not know well. In Italy students use this form with teachers.

When talking to one person

Familiar (tu)   Polite (Lei)
Come stai?

Dove abiti?

(Tu) sei italiano/a?		 How are you?

Where do you live?

Are you Italian?		 Come sta?

Dove abita?

Lei è italiano/a?
The familiar form uses the 2nd person singular form of the verb (tu)   The polite form uses the 3rd person singular form of the verb (lui/lei/Lei)

When talking to a group

Familiar (voi)   Polite (Loro)
Come state?

Dove abitate?

(Voi) siete italiani/e?		 How are you (all)?

Where do you (all) live?

Are you (all) Italian?		 Come stanno?

Dove abitano?

Loro sono italiani/e?
The familiar form uses the 2nd person plural form of the verb (voi)   The polite form uses the 3rd person plural form of the verb (loro/Loro)

Possiamo darci del tu?
When you have got to know an Italian better he or she may suggest, 'Possiamo darci del tu?', which figuratively means 'May we switch to the tu form?'.

Use of titles in Italian

Apart from the common titles signore, signora, signorina, there are other titles that are commonly used by Italians especially in business and formal situations.

Dottore/Dottoressa – as well as a doctor, any one with a university degree can use this title before their name.

Ingegnere – used to refer to someone who has a degree in engineering or related areas including scientific and technological fields.

Professore/Professoressa – teachers of middle school, high school and university can use this title. It is also used for musicians who play in an orchestra.

Avvocato/Avvocatessa – the title used by lawyers.
