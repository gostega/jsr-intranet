Nazionalità

Christian says Sono australiano. Lucia says Sono australiana.

The endings are o for Christian (masculine) and a for Lucia (femiinine), respectively.

Why are the endings different?
This is because nationalities are aggettivi.
In Italian, the adjective ending changes to agree with who or what is being spoken about.

The –o and –a endings for nazionalità are used as follows:
–o refers to a male person
–a refers to a female person.

Now look at the following:
Emily says Sono inglese.
You would say the same for a boy.
Peter è inglese.

Why is inglese the same for both male and female?
This is because the adjective (the nationality) ends in –e. In this case the word will be the same for both genders.

Did you notice that in italiano capitals are not used when speaking about nazionalità.

Origini

Ways to express where a person originates from are:
Pierluigi è di *Roma.

Pierluigi è romano.

Laura viene da Perth.

Lei è d'origine italiana.

La mia famiglia viene dalla Francia.

*Romani with a capital 'R' is also used to refer to the ancient Romans – i Romani.
