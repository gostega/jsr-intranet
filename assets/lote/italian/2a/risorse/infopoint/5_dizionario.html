Il dizionario

Why use a bilingual dictionary?

You can use a bilingual dictionary to:

  • look up an Italian word you have heard/read whose meaning you don’t know
  • look up an Italian word for your own use in speaking/writing
  • check the gender of an Italian word
  • check the spelling of known Italian words
  • check the verb tables to help you in your writing.
What will a bilingual dictionary not give you?

A bilingual dictionary will not give you:

  • all the parts of a verb – only the infinitive
  • all the tenses of a verb – usually only selected forms of irregular verbs are given
  • plural and feminine forms of regular adjectives (ones that follow normal rules)
  • the plural form of most nouns
  • lots of different meanings (particularly small dictionaries).
How to use a dictionary effectively

When looking for a word in the Italian-to-English section When looking for a word in the English-to-Italian section
  • When reading or listening to a text, do not look up every word that you don’t know. Guess the meaning from the context (the sentence or paragraph in which the word appears).
  • Read through all the dictionary entries to have the best possible chance of getting the correct meaning for that context in English.
  • After looking up the meaning of an Italian word, ensure that you have expressed your answer in good English. A string of words found in a dictionary does not constitute a correct answer.
  • Read through all the entries to give yourself the best possible chance of choosing the correct expression for your purpose.
  • Take note of the part of speech so that you can make sure that you have chosen the correct word. Many words can have different roles in a sentence and can therefore be different parts of speech, for example a word can be a noun and a verb, depending on the context.
  • Take note of familiar words and vulgar words and use them only when appropriate.

To refresh your memory:
  • Noun is the name given to a person, place or object.
  • Adjective is used to describe a noun.
  • Verb is an action.
  • Preposition is a joining word.
  • Adverb is used to modify a verb or an adjective.

Strategies for learning new vocabulary

Decide if a new word is one that you will probably use again. If it is, record the word somewhere.

Organise new words on cards, in a notebook or on your computer either:

  • by theme, or
  • alphabetically.

    • Remember that the gender is part of a word. Use either:

  • the definite/indefinite article, or
  • (m)/(f) after the word.

    • Write new words in a phrase or sentence either:

  • from the context in which you came across it, or
  • write your own context.

    • Review your vocabulary frequently, not just before tests or exams.

    Get someone to test you, or set yourself a test, for example remembering 10 expressions related to a theme that you have studied.
