Both Enrico and Valentina used the phrase mi piace ... to talk about their hobbies.

Mi piace can be used before an infinitive verb and a noun to express 'I like to do something'.

mi piace + infinitive verb mi piace + noun

Mi piace studiare!

Mi piace fare nuove amicizie.

Mi piace la musica.

Mi piace il calcio.

Refer to Elementi linguistici – Il verbo piacere to find out more about how to express likes and dislikes.

Look at the following.

Mi piace il calcio.Mi piace la musica.

il and la both mean 'the'. They are two of the seven forms Italian has for 'the'. These are known as definite articles.

Refer to Elementi linguistici – Il genere dei sostantivi and L’articolo determinativo e L’articolo indeterminativo to review gender of nouns, definite and indefinite articles.

What else did Enrico and Valentina say about themselves? Look at the Commenti section.

Enrico described himself as un ragazzo *simpatico amichevole ma a volte testardo. Valentina described herself as una ragazza *sensibile, disponibile e dolce.

*simpatico is an adjective that is not easily translated into English. It means nice, friendly or charming. There is no one English word that corresponds directly.

The opposite is antipatico and it basically means unpleasant.

You may be tempted to think that *sensibile translates into English as ‘sensible’.

Sensibile actually means 'sensitive'! This is a 'false friend' as it looks like an English word but doesn't have the same meaning.

Refer to Falsi amici under Come si dice? for an extensive list of false friends.
