La famiglia

Immagina!

You are going to Italy and will be staying with an Italian family. While you are there, you will want to talk about your family. This will help you to cope with those moments of nostalgia!

Prepare a photo album of your family to take with you. Include captions.

  • Descrivi la tua famiglia.
    Provide descriptions of all family members. Include information about your family relationships.
  • Vai d’accordo con la tua famiglia?
    How do you see your relationship with your family members?

For ideas on how to create a digital album, go to http://myphotobook.it.

Other options to present la famiglia include:

  • a slideshow presentation, for example PowerPoint®
  • a photo CD/DVD with commentary
  • an appropriate form of your choice.

Use the language that you have just learned through the Discover and Engage sections to help you with this task. Remember that you can modify and manipulate phrases to suit your needs!

Discovering the link: Un’attività di commercio italiana in Australia

Interview an Italian person who has established a business in Australia recently. This will allow you to focus on modern-day trends.


The reason Italians have for coming to Australia today is quite different from previous generations.

An account of an Italian migrant’s experience can be found at: www.museum.vic.gov.au >> Education >> ed-online >> Hear Her Voice >> Maria D'Astoli Italy.


Un’attività di commercio

Select an Italian business/company that has recently been established in Australia.

Examples can be found by contacting the ICCI, or you may also have contacts from personal experience.

Contact the business that you have selected to arrange an interview.

Alternatively, you may choose to interview an Italian who has come to work in Australia recently.

Domande da fare

Prepare interview questions to ask in Italian.

The interview should focus on the following areas:

  • why the person came to Australia
  • how the business venture/work is going
  • what the person sees as the advantages and disadvantages of operating a business/working in Australia.

Here are some sample questions to help you get started.

Quando è venuto/a in Australia? Da quanto tempo è in Australia?
Perché ha scelto di venire in Australia?
Mi puo’ parlare del lavoro che fa?
Come va il lavoro/l’azienda?
Quali sono i vantaggi di lavorare qui?
C’è una grande richiesta/un mercato per il Suo prodotto?
Quali sono i punti negativi?
Quali difficoltà ha affrontato in Australia?
Le manca l’Italia?
Pensa di ritornare in Italia?

This is a formal situation. You need to use the polite (formal) form of language, for example Lei form. The interview with the ICCI in Attività 7 provides you with an example of how to do this.

Refer to Elementi linguistici – Pronomi informali e formali to review how this is done.

L’intervista

This is an opportunity for you to use your knowledge of Italian in a real-life context and to communicate with an Italian person.

During the interview, focus on the questions that you have prepared. However, as this is not a scripted role play but a real-life situation, you need to be flexible.
