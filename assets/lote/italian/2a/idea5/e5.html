Attività 5 Gli insegnanti possono essere amici?

Read what these students have to say about teachers on the following forum site.

The verbs dovere potere and volere are known as i verbi servili as they are followed by another verb in the infinitive.

There are many examples of i verbi servili in the comments on the forum site. For example:

deve conoscere bene la materia

deve essere simpatico

Refer to Elementi linguisici – Il verbi riflessivi to review how reflexive verbs are formed.

Use the highlighter to identify any modal verbs.


  • Italian students often refer to their teacher as il/la prof.
  • The expression sia ... sia means both ... and sia in classe sia a casa

Look at the following phrases. Select the forum entry in which these views were expressed.

Purtroppo quest’anno ho molti insegnanti con i quali non ho potuto instaurare nessun rapporto.

In this sentence con i quali translates as ‘with whom’.

Quale is one of i pronomi relativi. Other relative pronouns include che and cui.

Refer to Elementi linguisici – I pronomi relativi for further details.

Parliamo della scuola

Your Italian class has established an online link with a class in another school.

A chat session has been scheduled and the topic for your discussion is i professori.

You have been given the following questions as a guideline for the conversation.

  • Come sono i tuoi professori?
  • Vai d’accordo con i tuoi professori?
  • Quali sono i professori che non ti piacciono? Perché?
  • Secondo te, come dovrebbe essere l’insegnante ideale?

Print this worksheet to record Le risposte dell’altra persona – Appunti.


Preparation for the chat session is important. Think about how you will answer the questions and jot down some notes.

During the chat, make a note of what the other student says in italiano.

The questions are a guideline and will help to define what you talk about. Keep in mind, however, that it’s a conversation and not a scripted role play. Therefore, you need to allow for some flexibility. It’s important to listen to the other person and respond to cues.


Refer to InfoPoint – Speaking strategies for some useful tips to help you with this attività.
