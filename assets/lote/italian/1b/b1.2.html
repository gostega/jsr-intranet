Fare sport means ‘to do sport‘.

Lo sport does not change its ending in the plural form.

Refer to Plurali irregolari for other examples of irregular plurals.

Giocare

The verb giocare is used to speak about playing a sport.

Gioco a basket. I play basketball.
Giochi a calcio? Do you play soccer?

Notice how giocare adds an ‘h’ to the tu form. This is to retain the hard ‘k’ sound of the infinitive verb. This also is the case for the noi form.

The reason for this is because the ‘i’ of the tu and noi endings coming after the ‘c’ would create a soft ci – ‘chi’ sound in the middle of the verb.

Fare

Fare can be used with sporting and physical activities.

Lei fa yoga. She does yoga.
Lui fa surf. He does surfing.
Io faccio tennis e hockey. I do tennis and hockey.
Loro fanno atletica leggera. They do athletics.

The definite article is not included after fare.
