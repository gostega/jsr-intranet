Lo sai che

Italian cinema today, like the music scene, is a mixture of Italian and English films. There is little difference in the films shown in Italy and in Australia. The timing of the release of the film may sometimes vary.

In Italy, English films (and in fact most foreign films) are dubbed, whereas in Australia, foreign films have subtitles.

Italians are very accustomed to watching dubbed films. As you can imagine, the dialogue does not correspond with the mouth movements.

If you have Internet access, go to Extra: Il cinema in Italia. This will give you an idea of the films being screened at the moment in Italy. You will be surprised to see that in actual fact, Italian teenagers are watching the same films as you!
