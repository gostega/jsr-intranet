L’articolo determinativo

Did you notice the use of il, la, i, le, gli in the examples? These words are used before nouns and mean ‘the’. They are called definite articles. In English the definite article has only one form: the. In Italian l’articolo determinativo has seven different forms according to gender, and whether the noun is singular or plural.

Masculine
Il singolare
Il plurale
used before words beginning with a consonant, eg
il il ragazzo (the boy)
il libro (the book)
il motorino (the scooter)
 i i ragazzi (the boys)
i libri (the books)
i motorini (the scooters)
used before words beginning with a vowel, eg
l’ l’italiano (the Italian)
l’amico (the male friend)		 gli gli italiani (the Italians)
gli amici (the male friends)
used before words beginning with s followed by a consonant or z, eg
lo lo studente (the student)
lo zodiaco (the zodiac)		 gli gli studenti (the students)
gli zodiaci (the zodiacs)

Feminine
Il singolare
Il plurale
used before words beginning with a consonant, eg
la la ragazza (the girl)
la scarpa (the shoe) 		le le ragazze (the girls)
le scarpe (the shoes)
used before words beginning with a vowel, eg
l’ l’arte (the art)
l’amica (the female friend)		 le le arti (the arts)
le amiche (the female friends)

Note:
 • l’ is used for both masculine and feminine singular nouns.
• l’ change to gli for masculine plural nouns and le for feminine plural nouns.
