Il genere dei sostantivi – Il plurale

You have already learned that nouns in Italian have a gender, ie that they can be either masculine or feminine.

Remember that:
• most Italian words end in a vowel
• the gender of the noun is usually indicated by the vowel at the end of the word.

Nouns, as well as having a gender, can be either singular (when they refer to one thing) or plural (when they refer to more than one thing). You have already learnt about the singular of nouns. Now we will look at the plurals of nouns.

Maschile
Masculine nouns become plural by changing the ending from –o to –i. Per esempio:
ragazzo
amico
italiano		 ragazzi
amici
italiani
Femminìle
Femimine nouns become plural by changing the ending from –a to –e. Per esempio:
ragazza
italiana
festa		 ragazze
italiane
feste
Maschile o femminile

Masculine and feminine nouns which end in –e in the singular change to –i in the plural.

studente studenti (m)
cellulare cellulari (m)
arte arti (f)

There are other exceptions to these rules. These will be looked at in later sections as the need arises.
English words that are commonly used in Italian or words that do not end with a vowel do not follow this pattern. The ending of the noun does not change. Look at the following examples:
