I verbi riflessivi

Reflexive verbs are used to describe actions which the subject is performing on himself/herself. In English they are used with the words such as ‘myself’ or ‘himself’, for example I wash myself. In Italian they are used with reflexive pronouns. Study the reflexive pronouns below.

Singular Plural
mi myself ci ourselves
ti yourself vi yourselves
si himself, herself, itself, yourself (polite) si themselves, yourselves (polite)

The infi nitive form of refl exive verbs ends in –si. For example:

allenarsi
vedersi
divertirsi

How reflexive verbs are formed

They follow the normal patterns of regular –are, –ere and –ire verbs.

  1. Remove the –arsi, –ersi and –irsi at the end of the verb. (The endings that precede the –si indicate whether they are –are, –ere or –ire verbs.)
    2. allenarsi (–are) --> allen
    vedersi (–ere) --> ved
    divertirsi (–ire) --> divert
  2. Add the appropriate endings.
  3. Include the appropriate refl exive pronoun before the verb.

Look at the following tables to see how this is done. The verbs are conjugated in the present tense.

allenarsi
io mi alleno I train
tu ti alleni you train
lui si allena he trains
lei si allena she trains
noi ci alleniamo we train
voi vi allenate you train (plural)
loro si allenano they train
Formal
Lei si allena you train
Loro si allenano you train (plural)
vedersi
io mi vedo I see myself
tu ti vedi you see yourself
lui si vede he sees himself
lei si vede she sees herself
noi ci vediamo we see each other
voi vi vedete you see each other (plural)
loro si vedono they see each other
Formal
Lei si vede you see yourself
Loro si vedono you see yourself (plural)
divertirsi
io mi diverto I enjoy myself
tu ti diverti you enjoy yourself
lui si diverte he enjoys himself
lei si diverte she enjoys herself
noi ci divertiamo we enjoy ourselves
voi vi divertite you enjoy yourselves (plural)
loro si divertono they enjoy themselves
Formal
Lei si diverte you enjoy yourself
Loro si divertono you enjoy yourself (plural)

Notice that the addition of the reflexive pronouns is the only thing that is new. The verb endings remain the same.

Many verbs can have both the normal and the reflexive form. For example:
chiamarsi Si chiama Enrico.
The action is reflected back on the subject.
chiamare Chiamo Enrico per invitarlo alla festa.
In this case io (I) is the subject who is calling Enrico.

**mantenersi is an irregular verb. It is similar to the verb tenere.

The past tense of reflexive verbs

When conjugating reflexive verbs in the passato prossimo, the auxiliary verb essere is always used. Look at the example.

allenarsi
io mi sono allenato/a I trained
tu ti sei allenato/a you trained
lui si è allenato he trained
lei si è allenata she trained
noi ci siamo allenati/e we trained
voi vi siete allenati/e you trained
loro si sono allenati/e they trained

Refer to Il passato prossimo if you need to revise how to form the past tense with essere.
