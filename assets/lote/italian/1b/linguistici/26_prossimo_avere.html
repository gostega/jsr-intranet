Il passato prossimo (avere)

In Italian il passato prossimo is used to refer to past events, particularly those which have occurred in the recent past.

The passato prossimo is formed by using two verbs.
Look at the example:

Ho comes from avere and is known as the auxiliary (helping) verb. Ho mangiato. Mangiato comes from the infinitive mangiare and is known as the past participle.

The passato prossimo is formed in the following way:

Step 1: Take the infinitive verb and remove the infi nitive ending:

parlare --> parl
ricevere --> ricev
dormire --> dorm

Step 2: Some verbs have regular past participles. They are formed by adding the following endings:

ato for -are verbs --> parlato
uto for -ere verbs --> ricevuto
ire for -ire verbs --> dormito
This is known as the past participle for regular verbs.

Step 3: Now add the auxiliary (helping) verb avere.

Most verbs in the passato prossimo use avere as the auxiliary (helping) verb. In general *transitive verbs are conjugated with avere.

*Transitive verbs are identified in the dictionary with an italic vt.

The following are some examples of the passato prossimo using avere. You will notice that the past participle always stays the same.

Parlare To speak
io ho parlato I have spoken/spoke
tu hai parlato you have spoken/spoke
lui/lei ha parlato he/she has spoken/spoke
noi abbiamo parlato we have spoken/spoke
voi avete parlato you (plural) have spoken/spoke
loro hanno parlato they have spoken/spoke
Ricevere To receive
io ho ricevuto I have received/received
tu hai ricevuto you have received/received
lui/lei ha ricevuto he/she has received/received
noi abbiamo ricevuto we have received/received
voi avete ricevuto you (plural) have received/received
loro hanno ricevuto they have received/received
Dormire To sleep
io ho dormito I have slept/slept
tu hai dormito you have slept/slept
lui/lei ha dormito he/she has slept/slept
noi abbiamo dormito we have slept/slept
voi avete dormito you (plural) have slept/slept
loro hanno dormito they have slept/slept
