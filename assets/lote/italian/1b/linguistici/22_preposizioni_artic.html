Preposizioni articolate

Generally, when le preposizioni semplice (di, a, da, in, su) are followed by the definite article, they join together to form a compound preposition. Look at the following example.
The preposition a means to, in, at. When followed by the definite article, it changes in the following way.

With the preposition con the separate form is more commonly used.
Per esempio:

Vado in città con gli amici. - I go to the city with friends.

Mi piace parlare con gli amici. - I like to talk with friends.
