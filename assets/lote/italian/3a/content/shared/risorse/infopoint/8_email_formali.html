Lettere/e-mail formali

Letters/emails that you write to people you do not know very well or people in positions of power will generally be in formal language.

When writing a formal letter/email, you should keep the following in mind.

Use:

  • the Lei form
  • Gentile, Gentilissimo a/i/e Egregio to begin the letter/email – this is the English equivalent of ‘Dear’
  • one of the following ways to conclude the letter/email

    Cordiali saluti,
    Le invio i miei migliori saluti
    i più cordiali saluti.
    Le invio i migliori auguri
    un cortese saluto
    distini saluti

    These are the English equivalents of ‘Regards’
  • language that is formal and precise – do not use language that reflects the informal nature used in conversation.
