Le preposizioni semplice

A preposition is a word that precedes a noun or a pronoun and expresses that word's relation to another word.

The following is a list of common simple prepositions:

a to, in, at
di of, from, about, made by
da from, at, to
in in, at, to the, in the, at the, by
con with, by
su on

The English prepositions 'to' and 'in' are translated in Italian by 'a' when used with the name of a city or a small island.

(Io) vado a Sydney. I am going to Sydney.
(Lei) Abita a Lucca. She lives in Lucca.
(Noi) abitiamo a Rottnest. We live on Rottnest.

When referring to a country, region or a state the Italian 'in' is used.

(Noi) abitiamo in Australia. We live in Australia.
Lucca si trova in Toscana. Lucca is (found) in Tuscany.
