L’articolo indeterminativo

When you use a or an before a noun, you are using the indefinite article, for example, a bedroom, a house, a garden, an apartment.

In English an is used before nouns beginning with a vowel and a is used when the noun begins with a consonant.

In Italian there are four words for saying 'a/an'. They are as follows:

un Used with masculine words starting with a vowel or a consonant, for example:
        un giardino (a garden)
        un appartamento (an apartment)
        un amico (a male friend)
uno Used before masculine words beginning with s followed by a consonant, or masculine words beginning with z, for example:
        uno zoo (a zoo)
        uno studio (a study)
una Used with all feminine words beginning with a consonant, for example:
        una camera (a bedroom)
        una casa (a house)
un' Used with feminine nouns that begin with a vowel, for example:
        un'isola (an island)
        un'amica (a female friend)

These words can also be used to translate 'one' in Italian, for example:

un bagno (a/one bathroom)
The indefinite article is used only with singular nouns.
