L’imperfetto

L’imperfetto is another past tense. It’s used to speak about repeated actions and states in the past. It describes what used to happen or what was happening in the past.

L’imperfetto is formed by dropping the –re of the infinitive and adding the same endings to verbs of all conjugations.

Imperative of common regular verbs

  • Le forme tu, noi, voi
  parlare vedere partire finire
io parlavo vedevo partivo finivo
tu parlavi vedevi partivi finivi
lui/lei/Lei parlava vedeva partiva finiva
noi parlavamo vedevamo partivamo finivamo
voi parlavate vedevate partivate finivate
loro/Loro parlavano vedevano partivano finivano

–isc verbs in the imperfect follow the pattern of regular verbs.

L’imperfetto v il passato prossimo

L’imperfetto and il passato prossimo are both used to express actions in the past.

Il passato prossimo is used to speak about a completed action in the past.

Per esempio:

Ho finito i compiti ieri.I finished my homework yesterday.

Uses of l’imperfetto

  • This form of the verb expresses habitual and continuous actions in the past.

    Per esempio:  
    Ogni anno andavamo in vacanza all’estero. Every year we used to go on holiday overseas

  • describes past action or states that existed in the past; that is, physical, mental, emotional.

    Per esempio:  
    Mi sentivo molto felice. I felt very happy.

  • expresses weather, time and age in the past.

    Per esempio:  
    Era una bellissima giornata e faceva bel tempo. It was a beautiful day and the weather was beautiful.

  • expresses age and habitual action.

    Per esempio:  
    Quando avevo otto anni nuotavo ogni giorno. When I was eight years old, I used to swim every day.

  • expresses incomplete actions in the past while something else was happening.

    Per esempio:  
    Marco suonava la chitarra mentre io cantavo. Mark played the guitar while I sang.

  • expresses incomplete actions in the past when something else happened.

    Per esempio:  
    Io guardavo la televisione quando mia madre è arrivata a casa. I was watching TV when my mother arrived home.
Notice how il passato prossimo (present perfect) is used with l’imperfetto.
Il passato prossimo is used for the action that happened – that is, it is complete whereas l’imperfetto is used to translate the longer action – that is, it was in progress before, during and possibly after the other action happened. Look at the diagram below.
  • The imperfect is also used after conjunctions like mentre/quando.

    Per esempio:  
    Mentre io uscivo la sera lui stava a casa a studiare. While I went out every night, he stayed home and studied.
