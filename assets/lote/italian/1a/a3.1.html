Mi piace

Both Francesca and Riccardo used mi piace when talking about tempo libero. Let’s have a look at how it was used in their profiles.

It can be used directly before a noun.
Mi piace la musica - I like music.
Mi piace l’arte - I like art.

It can be used before a verb in the infinitive to mean ‘I like to do something’.
Mi piace uscire con gli amici - I like to go out with friends.
Mi piace comunicare in inglese - I like to communicate in English.

To say that you don’t like something, all you need to do is add a non in front of mi piace.
Non mi piace lo sport. - I don’t like sport.

Amo … Adoro…

Amo and Adoro were also used by Francesca and Riccardo when speaking about tempo libero.

  • Amo comes from the verb amare.
  • Adorare comes from the verb adorare.

In Italian the literal meaning of morire is ‘to die’. When gli italiani use the expression amo da morire, it has nothing to do with death! What they are really saying is that they absolutely love something!
