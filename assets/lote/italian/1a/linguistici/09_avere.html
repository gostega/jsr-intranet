Avere

The italian verb avere means 'to have'. It is a very common irregular verb. You need to memorise all its forms. Avere is conjugated in the following way.

(io) ho I have
(tu) hai you (singular) have
(lui) ha he has
(lei) ha she has
(noi) abbiamo we have
(voi) avete you (plural) have
(loro) hanno they have
Formal
Lei ha you (singular) have
Loro hanno you (plural) have

In italiano the verb avere is used when speaking about age.
In inglese the verb ‘to be’ is used to speak about age.

Expressions with avere

Ho sedici anni. I am 16.
Quanti anni hai? How old are you? (informal)
Quanti anni ha? How old are you (formal)

Here are some common expressions in italiano that use the verb avere. However, in inglese the verb ‘to be’ is used to translate the expressions.

avere caldo to be hot
avere freddo to be cold
avere fame to be hungry
avere sete to be thirsty
avere fretta to be in a hurry
avere ragione to be right
avere torto to be wrong
avere paura to be scared
