Essere

Sei and sono come from the verb essere which means 'to be'.
To talk about yourself and others, you need to know the verb essere. It is a very common irregular verb. You need to memorise all its forms.

Essere is conjugated like this:

(io) sono I am
(tu) sei you (singular) are
(lui) è he is
(lei) è she is
(noi) siamo we are
(voi) siete you (plural) are
(loro) sono they are
Formal
Lei è you (singular) are
Loro sono you (plural) are

 

The words in red io, tu, etc are called i pronomi personali.

(io) I
(tu) you (singular)
(lui) he/it
(lei) she/it
(noi) we
(voi) you (plural)
(loro) They
Formal  
Lei you (singular)
Loro you (plural)

They are always used before the verb in English but can be left out in Italian. Per esempio:

Io sono Richard. or Sono Richard.
Io sono australiano. or Sono australiano.

This is because each personal pronoun has a different verb ending. Therefore, this makes it clear who the subject of the verb is. Personal pronouns are generally used when you want to emphasise the person who is speaking or being spoken about.
