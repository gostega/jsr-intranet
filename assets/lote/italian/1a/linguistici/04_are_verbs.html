Il presente of -are verbs

The present tense is used to speak about what you do or are doing.

Italian verbs fall into three groups:
  -are abitare to live
  -ere scrivere to write
  -ire dormire to sleep
This form is know as the infinitive form.

The infinitive is the basic form of the verb. It is the form that you will find in the dictionary.

Regular verbs in each group have a pattern that they follow. Once you have learnt the endings for regular verbs in the present tense, you can use any other regular verb. This is because they follow a regular pattern.

Verbs that end in -are are the most frequently used in Italian.

How to conjugate (form) an -are verb:
Step 1: Drop the -are at the end of the verb.
Step 2: Replace the -are with a different ending for each subject pronoun (person).

Let’s conjugate the verb:

abitare
(io) abito I live
(tu) abiti you live
(lui) abita he lives
(lei) abita she lives
(noi) abitiamo we live
(voi) abitate you (plural) live
(loro) abitano they live
Formal
Lei abita you live
Loro abitano you (plural) live
parlare
(io) parlo I speak
(tu) parli you speak
(lui) parla he speaks
(lei) parla you speak
(noi) parliamo we speak
(voi) parlate you (plural) speak
(loro) parlano they speak
Formal
Lei parla you speak
Loro parlano you (plural) speak
