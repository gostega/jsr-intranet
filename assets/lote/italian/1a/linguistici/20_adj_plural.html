Aggettivi - Il plurale

Adjectives that end in –o in their original form# change to –i for masculine words and to –e for feminine words.

1. Adjectives that end in –o in their original form#.

Maschile
o -> i
Femminite
a -> e
(Loro sono) bello -> belli
carino -> carini
italiano* -> italiani*
australiano* -> australiani*		 (Loro sono ) bella -> belle
carina -> carine
italiana* -> italiane*
australiana* -> australiane*
* Note that in Italian capitals are not used for nationalities.

2. Adjectives that end in –e change to –i in the plural form for both masculine and feminine words.

Maschile
e -> i
Femminite
e ->i
(Loro sono) dolce -> dolci
socievole -> socievoli
vivace -> vivaci		 (Loro sono ) dolce -> dolci
socievole -> socievoli
vivace -> vivaci

# Original form in the context of this course refers to how words appear in the dictionary. For adjectives this is the singular masculine form.

You describe an object in exactly the same way as you describe a person. First of all, you need to work out whether the word is masculine or feminine, ie the gender. When you have chosen the aggettivo, you then change the ending to agree with the gender of the word.
