I saluti

Ciao means both 'hello/hi' and 'bye'. It is a greeting that is now widely used also in Australia.
Italian teenagers use 'ciao' when greeting each other and in other informal situations with family and friends.
They use Buongiorno usually when greeting adults.

Relationships between young people and adults in Italy are more formal than in Australia. In Italian there are formal (also known as polite) and informal ways of addressing people. You will encounter the concept of formal and informal language often throughout Incontri and the course.

To find out more about ways of greeting people refer to I saluti.
