L’articolo determinativo

In English the definite article has only one form: the. In Italian l’articolo determinativo has seven different forms according to the gender of the noun it defines, and whether the noun is singular or plural.

Maschile
Il singolare
Il plurale
Used before words beginning with a consonant, for example:
il il ragazzo (the boy)
il libro (the book)
il motorino (the scooter)
 i i ragazzi (the boys)
i libri (the books)
i motorini (the scooters)
Used before words beginning with a vowel, for example:
l’ l’italiano (the Italian)
l’amico (the male friend)		 gli gli italiani (the Italians)
gli amici (the male friends)
Used before words beginning with s followed by a consonant or words beginning with z, for example:
lo lo studente (the student)
lo zodiaco (the zodiac)		 gli gli studenti (the students)
gli zodiaci (the zodiacs)

Femminile
Il singolare
Il plurale
Used before words beginning with a consonant, for example:
la la ragazza (the girl)
la scarpa (the shoe)		 le le ragazze (the girls)
le scarpe (the shoes)
Used before words beginning with a vowel, for example:
l’ l’arte (the art)
l’amica (the female friend)		 le le arti (the arts)
le amiche (the female friends)

Note:
  • l’ is used for both masculine and feminine singular nouns.
  • l’ changes to gli for masculine plural nouns and le for feminine plural nouns.

The two main elements that determine which definite article to use are:

  • the number: whether the noun is singular or plural
  • the gender: whether the noun is masculine or feminine.

In Italian, nouns are generally used with the definite article. In English the definite article is usually omitted. For example:

Mi piace il calcio. I like soccer.
(definite article il) (no definite article)
