I pronomi personali


The words in red io, tu, and the rest are called i pronomi personali.
io I
tu you (singular)
lui he/it
lei she/it
noi we
voi you (plural)
loro they
Formal  
Lei you (singular)
Loro you (plural)

They are always used before the verb in English but can be left out in Italian. Per esempio:

Io sono Richard. or Sono Richard.
Io sono australiano. or Sono australiano.

This is because each personal pronoun has a different verb ending, which makes it clear who the subject of the verb is. Personal pronouns are generally used when you want to emphasise the person who is the subject of the verb – whoever is speaking or being spoken about.
