Avverbi

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives and other adverbs. They do not agree with the word they modify.

Common adverbs refer to:

  • time
  • place
  • quantity
  • manner.

Many adverbs are formed by adding the suffix –mente to the feminine singular form of the adjective. This functions much like the English –ly. Per esempio:

lento -> lenta slow + mente -> lentamente slowly
attento -> attenta attentive + mente -> attentamente attentively

If the adjective ends in –e, you simply add –mente. Per esempio:

veloce quick + mente -> velocemente quickly

If the adjective ends in –le or –re, it drops the final –e before adding –mente. Per esempio:

facile -> facil easy + mente -> facilmente easily
probabile -> probabil probable + mente -> probabilmente probably
regolare -> regolar regular + mente -> regolarmente regularly

Avverbi irregolari

Some adverbs are irregular and do not follow this pattern.

bene well
male badly
molto very
poco little
troppo too much

Position of avverbi

In Italian, adverbs are usually placed after the verb. Per esempio:

  • Lorenzo legge attentamente i dépliant di viaggio.
                  verb        adverb

    (literally) Lorenzo reads attentively/carefully the travel brochure.
    However, in English, it is better to say:
    Lorenzo reads the travel brochure attentively/carefully.

With compound tenses, most adverbs go after the past participle.

  • Marta ha viaggiato molto l'anno scorso.
                 past participle adverb

    Marta travelled a lot last year.
