Text types

One of the important skills areas is that of writing a variety of texts in Italian.

As you are exposed to a wide range of texts, you will notice the different features of different text types. Apply what you learn to your own writing.

Hints on how to approach writing different text types.


  • Emails, notes, text messages, postcards and cards
    • These are usually short and information should be concise.
    • An email won’t have a date or an address.
    • A note usually only has the important pieces of information you wish to express.
  • Personal diaries, journals and blogs
    • Use fairly informal language, as if you were talking to a friend.
    • You may need to change between many different verb tenses.
  • Informal letter writing – for communication with family and friends
    • Include a greeting and a salutation, an address (if applicable).
    • The language in a letter suits the purpose of the letter and also the audience; that is, to whom you are writing. If you are writing to a close friend, you will probably use colloquial and even slang expressions.
  • Formal letters – letters to the editor and job applications
    • These letters will have introductory and closing paragraphs.
    • The language used is the polite/formal form of verbs and forms of address.
  • Instructions and recipes
    • To write instructions, the imperative tense is usually required.
  • Simple articles and advertisements
    • Identify the audience first as this will affect the language and structure you use.
    • An article for a school newsletter will feature more colloquial language than an article destined for the local paper.
  • Simple essays
    • They are used to describe events, to express ideas and to inform.
    • They include an introductory and a concluding paragraph, as well as paragraphs that make up the body of your essay.
    • Always check which verb tense you should use.

      Per esempio:

      • To express your opinion, you will probably use il presente.
      • To describe what happened on the weekend, use il passato prossimo and l’imperfetto.
      • To talk about your future dreams, use il futuro o il condizionale.

  • Conversations and dialogues
    • These will reflect spoken language. If you are in a formal situation, use the polite form of address. With a friend, use colloquial language.
    • Include enough language and extend your sentences. A dialogue that consists of short utterances will not give a true indication of your ability.
    • Use the conventions of writing a conversation. For example, you should indicate each time a different person speaks.
